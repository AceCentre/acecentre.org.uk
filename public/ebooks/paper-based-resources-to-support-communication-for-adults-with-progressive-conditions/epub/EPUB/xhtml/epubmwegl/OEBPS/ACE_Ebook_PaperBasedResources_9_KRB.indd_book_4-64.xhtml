This chapter shares information about sourcing alphabet charts and communication books (both free of charge and for purchase), and looks at software that can help you make your own paper-based resources. Although we have tried to include as many resources as we can, the lists of what’s available are not comprehensive.

With resources that you download and print at home, you might want to either laminate them or print them out on specialist paper. Laminating an alphabet chart or communication book makes it more robust and stops it getting tatty. For those pointing with a finger, it also makes the surface more slippery which can help with sliding your finger around. Alternatively, you could print a chart on so-called ‘indestructible’, tear-proof or waterproof printer paper.

When laminating, bear in mind that there are two types of laminate pouch available – gloss and matt. Gloss laminate tends to be the most common and widely used. However, it does reflect overhead lighting which can make it harder for you or your communication partner to see what is being pointed to. Matt laminate tends to work a bit better from this perspective and is just as robust and slippery.

The only proviso here is that if you are creating an eye pointing chart in the style of an E-tran frame or an eyelink style chart, gloss laminate is best as this is transparent once it has been through the laminator whereas matt laminate is slightly cloudy.

To locate resources made in languages other than English, you may want to consult the websites of specialist AAC centres in relevant countries. The International Society for Augmentative and Alternative Communication (isaac-online.org) may be a good place to start. The website lists their chapters around the world with contact details. The Speakbook contains an eye pointing alphabet chart and a chart that can be accessed by pointing in a growing number of languages (acecentre.org.uk/project/speakbook/ ).

Sourcing Communication Resources

Alphabet Charts - Freely Available

You can download lots of free alphabet charts from The Ace Centre website acecentre.org.uk/product-category/alphabet-charts/ There are options for pointing, high contrast colour schemes, encoded options, listener mediated scanning, coded charts and eye pointing charts.

AAC Scotland has an alphacard and QWERTY charts for download – see www.aacscotland.org.uk/downloads/.

The MNDA has a number of charts available to download – see mndassociation.org/professionals/management-of-mnd/aac-for-mnd/accessing-aac-equipment/aac-options-for-mnd/ and select the low tech option.

Letter and topic cueing alphabet charts can be downloaded from the ALS Augmentative Communication Program area of the Boston Children’s Hospital website - www.childrenshospital.org/centers-and-services/programs/a-_-e/als-augmentative-communication-program/protocol-of-assessment-considerations/letter-and-topic-cueing.

You can also download a listener mediated scanning alphabet chart from the Boston Children’s Hospital website - www.childrenshospital.org/centers-and-services/programs/a-_-e/als-augmentative-communication-program/protocol-of-assessment-considerations/quick-access-and-low-tech-tools. It is called called the AEIOU communication book.
