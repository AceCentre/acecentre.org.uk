This is where you hold your gaze briefly on the target letter then make eye contact with the communication partner to signal that the last letter looked at was your selection. This can work well if your eye movement is a little harder to read, or you find yourself looking around the frame a lot, as it gives the communication partner a little extra reassurance. SizeWhile the assumption is often that bigger is better, some people prefer to use an eye pointing system that is more discreet. Rather than A3 or A4, charts can be made in A5 size or even smaller.

Below is an example of a very small encoded chart. Rather than looking directly at the letters, with this system you look at and beyond them in the direction of the relevant arrow.

Others prefer to look round, make eye contact with the communication partner to signal that they are ready to select a letter, then briefly hold their gaze on the target letter. Whatever strategy is used, it is worth documenting it so that all know what is being done.

Such strategies often become redundant over time as you all gain confidence and familiarity in communicating with eye movement.
