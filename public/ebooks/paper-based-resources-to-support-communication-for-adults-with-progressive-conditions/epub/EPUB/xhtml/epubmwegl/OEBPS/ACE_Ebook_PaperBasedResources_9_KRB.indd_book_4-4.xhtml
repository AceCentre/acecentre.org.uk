The letters that Beatrice uses to spell her messages are in black permanent ink on the board. As she selects a letter, it is written in the centre of the board by Janet in blue dry erase pen.

Bridget has Motor Neurone Disease (MND). She can get her message across using speech most of the time. However, when she is tired or ill, issues and difficulties have begun to arise. She has started to explore different tools to support her speech, including an app on her phone and a small alphabet chart. Because she is still able to speak, it makes it easy for her to give her views when looking at different options. Everyone is different, but Bridget reflects that for her, “the fear of not being able to communicate at the end with motor neuron is horrible, but having the aids to communicate gives massive confidence and reassurance and also takes that fear away… it’s really given me great comfort to know that I can still communicate.”

 

Bridget’s feelings about communication aids

 

With conditions where communication needs can change over time, it may be worth doing some anticipatory work - learning how to use and maximise the potential of communication systems so that they are ready when needed.
