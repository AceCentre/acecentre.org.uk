Beatrice can vocalise or move her head to indicate ‘yes’ or ‘no’. She also uses a specific sideways head movement to indicate when a space is required. After letters that can potentially standalone, such as ‘A’ or ‘I’, Beatrice’s communication partners check whether or not the next letter selected is the start of a new word or the continuation of a word that begins with the letter ‘A’ or ‘I’.

The black letters on the board are written in permanent ink. As Beatrice selects a letter, her communication partner writes it on the whiteboard using a dry erase pen. If the communication partner writes an incorrect letter, Beatrice will vocalise or move her head to indicate that things have gone wrong.

You may have noticed that the letters on Beatrice’s whiteboard are not in alphabetical order. They have been grouped according to frequency of use, with the goal of letters that are used more often in the English language (like ‘T’) being quicker to access than those that are used less often (like ‘Z’).
