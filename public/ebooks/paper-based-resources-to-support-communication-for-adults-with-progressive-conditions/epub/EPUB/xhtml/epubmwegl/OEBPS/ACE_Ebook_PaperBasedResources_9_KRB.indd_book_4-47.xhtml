Small changes to an alphabet chart can make a big difference to usability. It is therefore worth spending some time finding the right alphabet chart for you. This chapter looks at the ways in which alphabet charts can differ and considers tweaks and modifications that can make them more effective.

This chapter looks at adjustments and personalisations such as:

• letter layouts

• chart size, font type and size and the amount of space between letters

• modifications for more significant visual issues

• including words and / or phrases, punctuation and numbers

• personalising the background of the chart

There are lots of charts that can be downloaded from the Internet, including from acecentre.org.uk. Alternatively, you may want to make your own. Chapter 12 contains lots of links to information about where to find alphabet charts and how to make your own.
