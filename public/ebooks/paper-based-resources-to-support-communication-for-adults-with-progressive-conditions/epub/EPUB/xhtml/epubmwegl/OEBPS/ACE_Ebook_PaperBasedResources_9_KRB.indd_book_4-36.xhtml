You can see Lorraine spelling out three letters on the Megabee in this video. For the letter ‘w’, she looks at the red block and then the black block as ‘w’ is situated in the red block and is itself coloured black. For the letter ‘e’ she looks at the blue block and then the black block; and finally for the letter ‘n she looks at the green block and then the yellow block. Spelling ‘when’ as ‘wen’ is a deliberate strategy Lorraine uses to speed up communication. She often simplifies words where possible.

﻿Eye Pointing Directly to Letters – ‘EyeLink’

Eye pointing to letters is most often done on an encoded E-tran frame. However, EyeLink is an alternative way of eye pointing to letters that doesn’t involve encoding.

These photographs show a customised EyeLink communication chart. It has been made by printing and cutting out individual letters and then laminating them to create the chart. An Eyelink chart can also be created by printing the alphabet on a piece of acetate.
