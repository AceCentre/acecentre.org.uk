The EyeLink chart pictured belongs to Lisa, who has cerebral palsy. This video shows her using her chart and explaining how it works. Note that Lisa chooses to look at the mirror image of the alphabet so that her communication partners can see it in alphabetical order. It is all down to personal preference.

As with all such tools, training and time for practise is important. For information about making or sourcing an EyeLink chart, see Chapter 12.

How to use the EyeLink Communication Method

 

The transparent EyeLink chart is held by the communication partner and you make eye contact through it. You then look at the letter you wish to select. The communication partner moves the chart in response to where you are looking, until you both end up looking straight ahead at the same letter. The communication partner speaks aloud the letter and you can indicate if a mistake has been made. Typically, you see the ‘correct’ layout (i.e. the letters in alphabetical order) and the communication partner sees the mirror image.
