Michael has just been introduced to using an alphabet chart with a frequency layout similar to the one shown in the video above. He is trying it out with Katherine, and, as you will see in the next video, there is a bit of sorting out to be done about how Michael communicates ‘yes that’s the letter I want’. In the end, Michael and Katherine agree that he will make eye contact with her when she says the letter he wants.

In the video, Katherine is holding a frequency layout alphabet chart that has a duplicate copy of the letters on the reverse. She has a pen and paper handy to enable her to keep track of what Michael is saying. This is probably just as well, as Katherine and Michael clearly don’t share the same taste in television programmes! Michael is happy for Katherine to try and predict the word he might be communicating.

Rather than speaking the first letter of the row aloud as in the tutorial video above, Katherine labels the rows ‘row 1, row 2’, etc. Although this is not shown on the video, as they become more confident in using this system together, Katherine and Michael agree to shorten this to ‘1, 2’, etc. Choosing to label the rows in this way is simply a matter of personal preference.
