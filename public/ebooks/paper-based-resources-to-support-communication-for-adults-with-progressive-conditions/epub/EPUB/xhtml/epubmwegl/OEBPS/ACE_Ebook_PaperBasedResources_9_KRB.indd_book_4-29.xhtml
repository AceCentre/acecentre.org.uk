If using hands is difficult, people occasionally choose to access an alphabet chart using head movement. This can either be done indirectly using an LED light pointer or directly using a headpointer. You can use this method with any type of alphabet chart, including an encoded or a coded chart.

A fairly discreet option is to attach an LED light pointer to a pair of glasses, a head band or a hat. You then gently move your head to direct the light beam so it highlights letters or words on a chart. As discussed above, LED pointers are designed to help presenters to highlight key points when delivering presentations and are therefore easy to source, although the head or glasses mounting is less standard. As far as we know, a pointer with a purpose built clip for use with glasses or a cap can only be purchased from a company in America (store.lowtechsolutions.org).
