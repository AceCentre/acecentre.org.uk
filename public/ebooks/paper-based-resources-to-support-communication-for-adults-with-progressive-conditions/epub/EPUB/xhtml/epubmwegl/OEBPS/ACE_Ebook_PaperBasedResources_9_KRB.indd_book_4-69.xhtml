Multiple System Atrophy (MSA)The Multiple System Atrophy Trust (www.msatrust.org.uk) has produced a leaflet that talks about aids that can help with communication (and more) https://www.msatrust.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/FS130-V1.2-Equipment-Communication-Eating-and-Drinking-1.pdf.

Parkinson’s DiseaseParkinson’s UK (www.parkinsons.org.uk) has information about communication issues associated with Parkinson’s Disease on their website. They also have a leaflet on Speech and communication problems in Parkinson’s that can be downloaded for free from their online shop https://shop.parkinsons.org.uk/collections/parkinsons-uk-information-1/products/communication-and-parkinson-s

Progressive Supranuclear Palsy (PSP) and Cortico Basal Degeneration (CBD)The PSP Association (www.pspassociation.org.uk) provides support for people with Progressive Supranuclear Palsy (PSP) and the related condition Cortico Basal Degeneration (CBD). They have produced a personal guide to PSP - section one contains information on speech and communication https://pspassociation.org.uk/information-and-support/just-been-diagnosed/pspa-publications/

Resources

https://assistivetechnology.org.uk is a website developed by the Specialised AAC and EC Services in England. It contains information about services available in England and is a repository of information and resources.

http://nowhearme.co.uk is a Scottish website that has been set up as part of the ‘Now hear me: It’s my right to speak’ campaign. The site is a gateway of information for people who use AAC, family members and professionals.

Back issues of Communication Matters journals are available to read online. These practical journals are published and distributed to members of Communication Matters (see below) three times a year. The back issues contain a wealth of information and can be downloaded here communicationmatters.org.uk/page/journals

You can also see links to huge amounts of information about AAC on the Internet through Ace Centre’s public library diigo.com/user/acecentre. This is where they keep a record of anything they stumble across on the Internet that seems interesting! They can also keep you up to date via their monthly newsletter - sign up at aacinfo.email.

There are a wide range of text books on AAC. A key text is Augmentative & Alternative Communication: Supporting Children and Adults with Complex Communication Needs (Fifth Revised Edition) by David Beukelman and Janice Light (2020). A well-known book written by someone using AAC is Jean-Dominique Bauby’s, The Diving Bell and the Butterfly. Bauby was a journalist, author and editor of French Elle magazine. He had a stroke in 1995 and dictated this book using movement in his left eyelid. The book was made into a film in 2007.
