Working with a Speech and Language Therapist is invaluable, as they will be able to tailor the information and support within this guide to meet your personal needs. They can help with the anticipatory work that will enable your communication needs to be met and can support you in modifying your tools as you become more experienced in using them.

If you are able to produce speech, a Speech and Language Therapist may also be able to help you explore message banking and / or voice banking.

Message banking is where you use your own voice to record stories, phrases, words and / or sounds which are saved and then played back to support your communication in time to come. When they are played back, the stories, phrases, words and / or sounds will sound exactly as they did when they were recorded. There are different ways of going about it, but some free options include an app called Record Me Now, a web-based service provided by Tobii Dynavox. Alternatively you could make use of the voice recorder on your phone. For ease, each story, phrase, word and / or sound should be recorded as a separate audio file.

Voice banking is slightly different. This is a process by which you create a personalised synthesised voice that should sound more like your existing voice than an ‘off-the-shelf’ voice. This personalised voice can be used to speak out anything that is inputted into an electronic communication aid. There is no way of knowing how the voice will sound until the process of creating it has been completed.

ACT have produced a useful guide about message and voice banking.

Finally, a Speech and Language Therapist can also support you in identifying and using an appropriate electronic communication aid and / or voice amplifier. If appropriate, they can assist you with accessing communication equipment funded by the NHS, or signpost you to alternative funding streams.

If you, or a family member or friend, are experiencing communication difficulties as a result of a progressive condition, then hopefully this guide

will give you some ideas of paper-based tools and strategies that you can try out. It is much better to get started with something and then tweak it, than to wait.
