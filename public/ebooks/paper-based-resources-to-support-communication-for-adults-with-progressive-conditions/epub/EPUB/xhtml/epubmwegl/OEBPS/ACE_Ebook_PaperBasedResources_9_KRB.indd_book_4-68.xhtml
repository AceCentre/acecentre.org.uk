Organisations – Communication

For support with communication, a great place to start is your local Speech and Language Therapy department. You will find details on your local NHS trust website.

Other organisations to consider include:

Ace CentreAce Centre provide help and expertise with AAC and assistive technology. They also offer free information and advice services. For more information see www.acecentre.org.uk or call their free telephone advice line on 0800 080 3115.

Communication Matters Communication Matters is a charitable organisation which covers the whole of the UK. They work to achieve a world where all individuals have a right to a voice through the provision of AAC equipment and services. The charity runs training events, a research programme, and produces a range of resources and publications. Its website is an up to date source of information for anyone wanting to learn more about AAC. For more information see communicationmatters.org.uk or call 0845 456 8211. Communication Matters is the UK branch of the International Society for Augmentative and Alternative Communication (ISAAC). See www.isaac-online.org

Local and National AAC Services You can locate your local AAC assessment centre in the UK using the Communication Matters website acecent.re/CM-assessAAC. You will also find information about national AAC services, and a wealth of other supporting information.

Organisations – Specific Conditions

There are a number of acquired progressive conditions that can impact upon speech. This section provides contact details for relevant organisations that can offer support and information:

Motor Neuron Disease (MND)The Motor Neuron Disease Association or MNDA (www.mndassociation.org) have lots of information about communication and AAC on their website and a range of alphabet charts for download. They have produced a leaflet around speech and communication support https://www.mndassociation.org/app/uploads/7C-Speech-and-communication-support.pdf. There is also more information in Section 8 of their Living with motor neuron disease guide https://www.mndassociation.org/support-and-information/information-resources/information-for-people-with-or-affected-by-mnd/living-with-mnd-guide/

Multiple SclerosisThe Multiple Sclerosis Society (www.mssociety.org.uk) has information about speech difficulties that are sometimes associated with Multiple Sclerosis on their website. They have also produced a booklet about this which can be downloaded here https://www.mssociety.org.uk/about-ms/signs-and-symptoms/speech/managing-speech-problems.
