Chapter 9 - Tweaks and Modifications That Can Make an Alphabet Chart More Effective

Including Useful Words, Phrases, Punctuation and Numbers

If there are messages or phrases that are being spelled out time and time again, or are just useful to have quick access to, these can be consolidated in a chart or book, and / or represented by a letter code. However, there may be a few key phrases or words that you choose to have printed on the alphabet chart itself.

Messages to flag up and deal with communication problems like ‘mistake’, ‘delete’ or ‘start over’ can be useful to have on a chart. A ‘space’ or ‘next word’ option makes it clear that you have reached the end of a word.

Punctuation can be included. A question mark and full stop are often sufficient, although some like to include other punctuation such as an exclamation mark and a comma.

Including numbers can be helpful as it saves spelling them out, although they do take up space.
