At Ace Centre we are privileged to work with some truly amazing people who need support with their communication for a wide variety of reasons. We are so grateful to the people who have generously allowed us to video them and have shared their stories and time to help us make this guide. Thank you.

We are also very grateful for the support of their families, friends and carers.

Since making the videos, there have been changes. Indeed, it is with great sadness that we share that some of those who helped us make this guide have passed away. Our thoughts are with their families, friends and carers.

For the purposes of this guide, we have made the decision to write about everyone involved in the present tense, describing their communication experiences as they were at the time of filming.

Chapter 1

﻿Introducing the People Featured in this Guide

Beatrice has had a diagnosis of Motor Neuron Disease (MND) for several years.

Having initially used her fingers to type out messages, she now uses her eyes to point to letters on a whiteboard and on a tablet computer. Beatrice uses the tablet to communicate if she is using it for other tasks, but for quick face-to-face communication, she tends to go first to her whiteboard system. Beatrice’s whiteboard is quite large, which works well at home.

When she goes out shopping, or on other outings, Beatrice has her carer pop a pen and notebook in her bag with the letters written out on the paper exactly as they appear on the whiteboard. This gives them a smaller, more portable system for when they are out and about. Through a combination of computer use, the customised white board, notebook backup, and Beatrice’s head movement and vocalisations, Beatrice is always able to get her message across, no matter where she is or how she is feeling.

﻿Beatrice
