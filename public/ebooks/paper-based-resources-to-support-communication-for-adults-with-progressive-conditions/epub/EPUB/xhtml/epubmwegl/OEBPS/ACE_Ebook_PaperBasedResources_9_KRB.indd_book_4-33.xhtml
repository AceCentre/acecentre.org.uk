Selecting Letters Using Eye PointingOne of the challenges of using eye pointing with an E-tran frame or chart can be differentiating between a searching look and a selecting look. In other words, how does the communication partner know whether you are still searching for the right letter or actually looking at your desired letter? The easiest way of signalling that something is being selected is to simply hold your gaze on it for a short period of time or until your communication partner responds.

This video shows another extract of Rosemary and Katharine using a Frenchay E-tran frame together for the first time. Rosemary’s eye pointing is clear to read. She simply holds her gaze fractionally longer on the desired letter or colour to indicate that she is making a selection.

The Eye Talk, designed by KM CAT (Kent and Medway Communication and Assitive Technology Service) breaks down the process of encoding a little further. This eye pointing chart is spread out over a number of pages which are bound together. You first look at the group that contains your target letter. Your communication partner then turns to another page which only displays the letters within your selected group. You then eye point to your chosen letter.
