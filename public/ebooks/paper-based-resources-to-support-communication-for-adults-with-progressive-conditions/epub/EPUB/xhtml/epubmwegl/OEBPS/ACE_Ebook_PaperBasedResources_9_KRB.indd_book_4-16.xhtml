Key Communication Partner Skills – a Summary

• Allowing enough time for you to say what you want to say

• Ensuring they fully understand how the communication system works, including how you communicate ‘yes’ and ‘no’

• Where appropriate, writing down each letter you have selected to help them keep track of the conversation

• Establishing whether you are happy for them to attempt to predict what you are saying, and if so, always checking if their guess is correct

• Staying calm if something goes wrong

• Avoiding the trap of double checking that each letter or word is correct before moving on to the next letter, but taking action if you indicate that they have gone wrong

• Where relevant, checking whether you have more to say before putting down the system

• Only speaking for you if asked

Things You Can Do to Help Your Communication Partners

Provide Instructions and Training if RequiredIt can be helpful to document how you communicate, particularly for less familiar communication partners. Clear instructions (sometimes known as a communication passport or interaction policy) can be printed on the tool itself or placed near you. You could also ask someone to use a smartphone to video you communicating to show to others.

As well as the mechanics of how the system works, the instructions could include information like how you will indicate you have something to say in the first place, how you will signal a space, or how you will let them know that something’s gone wrong.
