Bridget has MND and can get her message across using speech most of the time.

However, when she is tired or ill, issues and difficulties have begun to arise. For example, mid conversation with her husband, Bridget changed the topic to talk about the curtains. Her husband couldn’t decipher the word curtain, and didn’t manage to guess it, as it was a change of topic.

Bridget has started to explore different tools to support her speech, including an app on her phone and a small alphabet chart that is similar in size to her mobile phone screen. With either of these tools, she would have been able to spell out the word ‘curtain’. Her husband would have known what the topic change was, and their conversation could have continued verbally.

Dave has Multiple System Atrophy (MSA) and is unable to use speech to support his communication.

He is married to Pauline. If Pauline has a quick question, she will ask Dave to look to her right hand for ‘yes’ and her left hand for ‘no’. He has a tablet device controlled by a switch that he operates with his thumb. Specialist software highlights different options onscreen, and Dave presses a switch when the letter or other function that he wishes to select is highlighted. This is similar to the system that was used by Professor Stephen Hawking. Dave also uses an alphabet chart.

To use the alphabet chart, the communication partner points to each letter of the alphabet in turn and Dave puts his thumb up when the communication partner gets to the letter he wants to use (this is known as listener mediated scanning). That letter is then written down on a piece of paper to help the communication partner keep track of the conversation. Dave has had to spend time in hospital, and Pauline has ensured there are instructions by his bed so that nursing staff both understand his abilities and how he communicates. Pauline also put together some lists of frequently used messages that Dave could select from using the same thumbs up technique to quickly communicate with staff.
