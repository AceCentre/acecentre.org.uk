The FITALY keyboard is an example of a frequency layout designed for people who point using a finger. See fitaly.com for more information.

The FITALY layout has been designed to minimise the amount of movement required when spelling words using a single finger. The most frequently used letters are towards the centre of the chart, whilst less frequently used letters are around the edges. Although invented and patented by Jean Ichbiah for use with a computer or handheld device, the keyboard layout could also work well on a paper-based chart. Keyboard layouts designed for joystick use may also be of interest.

Frequency layouts are language dependent – the letter ‘Z’ is used much more frequently in Polish than it is in English, for example – and this will need to be considered if you need to communicate in more than one language. You may find it more efficient to have separate alphabet charts for each language if you opt to use a frequency approach.

If you find yourself having to create a frequency layout for another language, you will need information about the frequency of use of letters for that language. There is some information about this here on Wikipedia that may be a useful starting point, although it is not comprehensive.
