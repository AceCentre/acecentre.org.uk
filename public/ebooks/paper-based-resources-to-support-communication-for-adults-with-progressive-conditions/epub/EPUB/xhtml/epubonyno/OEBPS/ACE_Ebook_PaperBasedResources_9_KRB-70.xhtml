AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication): The term used to describe various methods of communication that can be used as an alternative or as an ‘add-on’ to speech where speech is not sufficient to enable successful communication in all situations all of the time. AAC includes simples systems such as pictures, gestures and pointing, as well as more complex techniques involving powerful computer technology.

ABC layout: The letters of the English alphabet are presented in alphabetical order on a keyboard.

Access method: Refers to the method by which an individual accesses their communication system. Also known as the selection method.

Alphabet chart: Refers to the method

Acquired condition: A condition or disease that you are not born with.

Automaticity: Describes motor activities that can be undertaken without much thought. For example, when first learning to drive, changing gear requires a lot of conscious thought. However,for an experienced driver, their body will just change the gear of the car without them having to think about what their foot is doing with the clutch or their hand is doing with the gear stick. Automaticity is acquired through repetition and practice.

Amplifier: A form of electronic communication aid that enhances the volume of speech.

Auditory Scanning: An access method that is a form of listener mediated scanning. The options are read aloud and the individual indicates when they have heard the target option.

ALS: Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) is also known as Lou Gehrig’s disease. It is more commonly called Motor Neurone Disease (MND) in the UK. It is a progressive neurodegenerative disease. The motor neurons degenerate and there is muscle wasting.

Abbreviation expansion: – see letter codes

Coded access: An access method where symbols are effectively given a grid reference that the individual then communicates. It requires two separate charts to communicate. One chart contains your symbols, the other allows you to communicate the location of the symbol you wish to communicate.

Combination access: An access method that involves a combination of two or more standard access methods.

Communication book: A book (usually printed) that contains words and / or phrases to support face-to-face communication.

Communication partner: A communication partner is anyone that a person using AAC communicates with.

Communication passport (LIFT)/ Interaction policyA rare progressive neurological condition. It is also known as Corticobasal Syndrome (CBS).

Corticobasal Degeneration (CBD): A rare progressive neurological condition. It is also known as Corticobasal Syndrome (CBS)

Electronic communication aid / High tech AAC: refers to a communication system that involves a battery or is computer based. The term is often used in contrast to low tech communication. It is a form of Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) Encoding.

E-tran frame / Eye transfer frame: An eye-transfer frame (or e-tran frame) is a low tech communication tool. It usually comprises a clear Perspex® frame to which symbols or letters are added, although it may be made of other materials such as laminate.
