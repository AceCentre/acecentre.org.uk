Alphabet Charts and Related Equipment - Resources available for purchase

The Stroke Association sells an alphabet chart pack that has been designed to help people with aphasia following a stroke who are able to spell. It includes both alphabetical and qwerty charts, numbers, days and months, and a clock with moveable hands. There are also illustrated sections covering How I am feeling, Personal care, Comfort, and Can I have. Although designed for people who have had strokes, the pack can be useful more widely. See https://shop.stroke.org.uk/shop/communication-aid-for-stroke-survivors-with-aphasia/.

Ace Centre has developed a range of encoded eye pointing charts that can be downloaded free of charge - https://acecentre.org.uk/product-category/alphabet-charts/alphabet-eye-point/. Some require access to a laminator to create them, but others can be printed and used without laminating.
