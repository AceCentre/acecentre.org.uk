Rosemary has MND and relies upon electronic tools to support her communication.

Before she had any specialist help, her children made her a simple chart of messages that she could eye point to. This proved to be a useful backup until other solutions were found to meet her communication needs. She has since been using the onscreen keyboard of an iPad with a voice output app, but as typing is becoming more difficult at times, she is also getting going with a computer that she controls with eye pointing.

We filmed her trying out an E-tran frame (the alphabet is displayed on a Perspex® board and you select letters by looking at them), and she felt that this would be useful to have as a backup to her electronic devices.
