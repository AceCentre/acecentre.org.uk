Rather than having separate tools, you could have an alphabet chart on one side and a communication chart on the back. If you are not able to turn the chart over yourself, make sure that you have a message on both sides that directs your communication partner to turn over the chart.

Communication Book

A communication book is a place where you can gather together a larger number of useful words and phrases.

A communication book can be separate from an alphabet chart. If you are using eye pointing or listener mediated scanning, it may be helpful to have a message on the alphabet chart signalling that you want to access the phrases, and a corresponding message within the book to indicate that you want to return to the spelling page. Alternatively, as in the examples below, you could bring the two together so that the alphabet chart is part of your communication book.

For a communication book to be useful, the phrases will need to be categorised in some way. Although the phrases could be presented in alphabetical order, it is often easier to identify category headings such as Chat, People, Care, Health issues, Feelings, Time / Events, Pets, etc. The categories chosen will be dependent upon you, your lifestyle and your communication needs. The categories needed, and their content, may also change over time.

A communication book can be produced in all shapes and sizes. A lot of the issues regarding font size, layout and presentation that are discussed in Chapter 9 in relation to alphabet charts, are also relevant to the production of a communication book. Ace Centre (and others) have produced several freely downloadable templates (see Chapter 12), but you may also choose to make your own.

As with alphabet charts, communication books can be set up to be accessed in different ways:

• By pointing with a finger or other body part

• Using eye movement

• Using listener mediated scanning

A communication book could also be created that is accessed by a combination of methods. For example, someone might eye point to a set of messages, and the communication partner might listener scan through the options.

Communication Books that can be Accessed by PointingCommunication books accessed by pointing can be created in lots of different ways. For example, they can be handwritten messages in a small notebook, printed pages within an A4 or A5 file or made using a downloadable template (see Chapter 12 for more information).
