Standard alphabet charts tend to be designed to be used with an index finger, although any part of the hand or indeed body can be used. They can be a good option to consider if writing is difficult. It may be worth keeping copies of your alphabet chart in different locations so that one is always to hand when needed.

Rather than spelling out the same thing repeatedly, try and keep a note of frequently used messages or phrases, as these can be delivered more efficiently. See Chapter 10 for more information.

Bridget is able to rely on speech to communicate most of the time. However, her speech does become less clear if she has been unwell or is tired. To deal with such situations, she uses an app on her phone to support her communication. She also has a small alphabet chart that is a similar size to her phone screen.

The alphabet chart’s small size really suits her and she finds it easier to use than a larger A4 sized chart. For her, one of the appeals of the alphabet chart is that it is lighter than her phone.
