Make sure there is Always a Copy of Your Communication Tool to HandOn a very practical note, consider having multiple copies of any system around the place so that there is always something to hand when you have something you need to say. Some find it helpful to keep a copy in every room of the house; others find it helpful to keep one in a handbag, pocket, with your mobile phone and / or permanently attached to any wheelchair.

Key Things You can do to Help Communication Partners – A Summary

• Documenting how you communicate, including how you communicate ‘yes’ and ‘no’, perhaps in a communication passport

• Allowing time for practise so new communication partners can gain confidence

• Ensuring pen and paper (or similar) is to hand

• Using key words or short sentences and /or simplified spelling if that suits you

• Making sure the system is always to hand, perhaps keeping copies in different locations
