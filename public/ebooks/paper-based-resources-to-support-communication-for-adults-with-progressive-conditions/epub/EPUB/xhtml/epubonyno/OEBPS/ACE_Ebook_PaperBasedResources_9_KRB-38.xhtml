The tools and strategies discussed up until now all involve directly selecting a letter, whether by pointing with a finger (or other body part), a pointing tool or pointing with eye movement. However, an alternative approach is for your communication partner to offer you the alphabet by pointing letter-by-letter and / or speaking the alphabet aloud. You then indicate when they point to and / or say your target letter.

This approach is known as listener mediated scanning or partner assisted scanning. In other words, the listener (i.e. the communication partner) scans through the alphabet for you. This video gives a flavour of how listener mediated scanning works:

To use listener mediated scanning, you need to be able to indicate ‘yes’ in some way. This could be through any agreed signal – it could be a vocalisation, a movement with part of the body like a hand, head or eye movement, or some other means. If it is a method that people who don’t know you might not immediately understand, it is worth documenting this to ensure that everyone is responding correctly to your signal.

The look of the chart can be adjusted and personalised to make it work better for you and your communication partners (you might increase the font size or adjust the colours for example) - see Chapter 9 for more information. Words and phrases that are regularly used may be accessed more efficiently using some of the strategies discussed in Chapter 10. As discussed in Chapter 2, you may find it speeds things up if you keep your sentences concise or even just spell out key words, but this is a personal decision.
