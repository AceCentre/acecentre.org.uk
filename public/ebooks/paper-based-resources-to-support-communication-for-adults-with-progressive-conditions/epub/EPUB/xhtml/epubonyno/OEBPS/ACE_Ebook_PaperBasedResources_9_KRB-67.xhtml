Making Your OwnWhile there are a lot of resources available to download or purchase, you may well find you want to make your own. A Speech and Language Therapist can support you in developing the most appropriate resource to meet your needs.

If you are creating resources for someone else in a language you are unfamiliar with, it is worth working with a native speaker of that language. Do bear in mind that if you are creating a frequency layout for another language, the frequency of use of letters for that language will differ to their frequency of use in English. There is some information on sites such as Wikipedia about this.

The simplest way of creating your own alphabet chart is to make a table within word processing or spreadsheet software. Similarly, when creating a communication book, you could either write messages in a notebook or use word processing software.

If you have access to specially designed resource making software (most likely this will be through your Speech and Language Therapist), you may find this offers a bit more flexibility when it comes to developing an alphabet chart. Some software also includes templates of alphabet charts. Examples of such software in the UK includes:

• The Boardmaker range of software e.g. available from tobiidynavox.com.

• Communicate in Print 3 e.g. available from widgit.com.

• Matrix Maker Plus e.g. available from inclusive.co.uk.

Such software is particularly useful if you need to create a communication book or chart with symbol support.
