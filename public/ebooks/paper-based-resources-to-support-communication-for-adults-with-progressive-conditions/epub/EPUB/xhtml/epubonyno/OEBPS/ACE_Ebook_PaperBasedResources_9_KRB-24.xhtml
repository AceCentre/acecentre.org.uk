Note that there are pointing tools that can be used with other parts of the body e.g. a head pointer.

An alternative pointing tool is an LED light pointer. Light pointers are designed to help presenters highlight key points when delivering presentations and are therefore easy to source. They can be used to select letters on an alphabet chart using head movement, but they can also be held in the hand.

These pictures give a flavour of how you might use a light pointer with an alphabet chart:

Using a KeyguardA keyguard is a cover that fits over a keyboard or computer screen and contains holes through which keys or areas of the screen can be selected. It allows an individual to rest their hand on the cover and make selections through the holes.

Some people find that using a keyguard with their alphabet chart makes it easier for their communication partner to work out which letter they are pointing to. They can be particularly beneficial if you experience tremor when pointing. FAB™ alphabet charts incorporate a keyguard into their design – see fab.uk.com, as does the Liberator Letter Board (liberator.co.uk).
