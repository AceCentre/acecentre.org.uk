﻿Designing Symbol Resources – More to Think About

In this chapter:

Look more closely at design and layout issues for symbol-based resources

Consider different ways colour can be used when designing symbol-based resources

Chapter 4 contains lots of exampes of communication charts. However, once you have got going with symbol-based AAC you may find that you need to make some changes and tweaks to the look of the chart to make them more useable for an individual.

There is a lot to think about when designing a communication chart and trial and error is an important part of the process! It is helpful to make careful observations about what seems to work and what does not, and to modify your chart design accordingly. As a general rule, try and involve the individual who will be using the chart in its design as much as is possible.

Labels

A symbol’s label can appear above or below the symbol, or even alongside it.

￼

The decision on where to put the label may be influenced by how someone selects the symbols on the chart. As the apple example below demonstrates, if someone is pointing to a symbol using a finger or fist, their hand may obscure a text label that is below a symbol. This can make things harder for the communication partner. Of course, if many of the communication partners are not literate then you might also want to make sure that they have as clear a view of the symbol as possible.
