Chapter 2 – More About Symbols

Symbols For People With Visual Issues

Some symbols that have been specially designed to be easier to see. PCS has a set of 1450 high contrast symbols that are designed for use by people with low vision or visual impairment.

There is more information about making symbol charts visually accessible in Chapter 7. However, you can read much more about visual accessibility, including tangible symbols, in Chapter 4 of Access to Paper-Based Symbol Resources When Pointing is Difficult.

High contrast PCS symbols alongside their standard counterpart

The AAC company Liberator also have a set of high contrast symbols. Although part of their LAMP package on their electronic devices, these symbols can be printed off and used elsewhere.

For people with complex visual difficulties, tangible or tactile symbols may be considered. These are symbols based around shapes and textures. Such symbols are often ‘home made’ as in the example below.
