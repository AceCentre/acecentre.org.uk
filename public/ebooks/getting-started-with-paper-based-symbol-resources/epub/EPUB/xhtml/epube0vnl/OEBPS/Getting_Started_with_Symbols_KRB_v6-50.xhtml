There is lots you can say on both charts! You can discuss what is in the garden, e.g. “look weed”, “uh oh weed grow” or “wow seeds grow”. You might describe what you see e.g. “like plant” or “finish grow”, and plan and carry out tasks in the garden e.g. “water plant”, “more water”, “you help rake”, “I stop dig” or “plant different seeds”.

Daily Routines

It’s great if you can try and bring AAC into some of your daily routines, as, by their very nature, they are things that happen regularly. These might include activities around self-care, chores (but for getting started, stick to enjoyable ones!), meal times (you could even use a place mat chart) or regular outings.

If you have access to a laminator or can tape a chart inside a plastic wallet, then bath time can be a great time to have fun with a communication chart with a child.
