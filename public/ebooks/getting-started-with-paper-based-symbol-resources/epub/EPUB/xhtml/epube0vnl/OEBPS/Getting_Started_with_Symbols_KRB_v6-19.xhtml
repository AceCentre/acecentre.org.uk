Chapter 3

﻿Choosing Vocabulary

In this chapter:

Think about what words to use on a chart

Consider core and fringe vocabulary

Think about multilingual resources

When creating your own charts, one of the first considerations is what to put on it! This chapter takes a look at the thinking behind what words to include.

Chapter 4 then provides lots of practical examples that can all be downloaded from Ace Centre’s website (acecentre.org.uk). For more indepth considerations about how the chart should look (e.g. size of symbols, colour, position etc.) see Chapter 6.

On Ace Centre’s website you will find a worksheet that will assist you in identifying the range of vocabulary needed for an individual and another that will help you think about the vocabulary needed for a specific topic or activity.

What Words Should be Used?

When getting started with AAC, the temptation is often to focus on making choices, but it is important to remember that communication involves so much more than just choosing or asking for things!

The video below is a good reminder of what communication is all about. Ruben is able to use his paper-based AAC to share a really special moment with his Mum.

Ruben is using a paper-based communication book that he accesses using auditory scanning. Ruben’s Mum reads aloud a series of options to Ruben, and he indicates “yes” when he hears the word or phrase that he wants to say. This method of accessing AAC is a form of listener mediated scanning. However, similar vocabulary could easily be presented on a symbol-based chart or book.
