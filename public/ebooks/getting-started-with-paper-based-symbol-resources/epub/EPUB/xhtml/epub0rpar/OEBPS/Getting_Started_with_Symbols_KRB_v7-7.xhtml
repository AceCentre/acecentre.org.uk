Chapter 1 – An Introduction to AAC

Some people will start their AAC journey using objects of reference and then move on to photographs, symbols, whole words and letters, or whatever works best for them. Some will use objects of reference alongside other ways of representing meaning. Others will always benefit from experiencing actual objects. Of course some people who use AAC may never require them.

CALL Scotland has produced an informative resource about objects of reference. Although aimed at schools, it’s a useful guide for anyone interested. The Brighton and Hove Learning Disability Partnership Board have also produced a helpful resource about objects of reference.

Electronic AAC is communication equipment that is powered and produces speech output. It is often based around computers, tablets or iPads. These may be wrapped in cases that contain additional amplification and / or battery power. The communication software / apps on these devices make use of computer generated / synthesised speech. There are many options available, in all sorts of shapes, sizes and specifications.

There are also some simpler electronic communication devices available that are not based around a computer, tablet or iPad. These devices store and play back a limited number of pre-recorded messages. They are sometimes referred to as light tech devices.

Representing Meaning

Words and concepts can be reperesented in aided AAC systems using objects of reference, symbols, whole words and letters.

Objects of Reference

Objects of reference are objects or things that are used to represent concepts. For example, a plastic cup could be used to represent the concept of having a drink or an armband could be used to represent going swimming. The object will need to be meaningful to the individual – an armband would not be an ideal object of reference for swimming for someone if they did not themselves use them or encounter them when swimming.

￼

As with all ways of representing meaning, objects of reference may be used to help with understanding and / or used by an individual to express themselves.
