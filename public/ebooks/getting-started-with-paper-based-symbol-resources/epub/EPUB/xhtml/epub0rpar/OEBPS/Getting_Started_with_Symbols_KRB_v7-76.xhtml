Chapter 9 – Next Steps – Developing a Communication Book

Published Resources

Communication charts and books are often developed in an ad hoc way. However, there are some resources that can help.

Developing and Using a Communication Book is a resource produced by Ace Centre to support families and practitioners when they are developing a paper-based communication book. It suggests a symbol-based core vocabulary that is developed over five stages and provides advice on how to support its use. It is available to purchase from ACE Centre – see acecentre.org.uk/resources/developing-using-communication-book/.

 

Look2Talk is a resource developed by Ace Centre to support families and practitioners in developing a paper-based communication book involving symbols for people who communicate using eye pointing. It shares a similar approach to the Developing and Using a Communication Book resource, but is optimised for eye pointing. It provides detailed guidance on how to set up, support and develop the use of eye pointing communication. It is available to purchase from Ace Centre – see

acecentre.org.uk/resources/look2talk.

￼

Low-tech support books are available from Liberator. The books complement a range of vocabulary packages found on their electronic communication aids, including easyChat Core, LAMP Words for Life and WordPower and are designed to be accessed by pointing. The books are free to download from liberator.co.uk. Manual boards (symbol charts) to complement vocabulary packages found on their Accent devices can also be downloaded.

PECS® (Picture Exchange Communication System), is a communication system that has been designed to support people who have autism or who are on the autistic spectrum and need to learn the purpose of communication. Symbols are picked up and given to communication partners. You can find out much more about PECS® at pecs-unitedkingdom.com.

The Pixon Project Kit is a communication and language curriculum that is based around a set of symbols called Pixons. While not resulting in a communication book, it is another symbol-based resource to be aware of. The kit consists of paper-based communication charts and teaching materials with a focus on core vocabulary. These are designed to be accessed by pointing. It is available from liberator.co.uk.

PODD (Pragmatic Organisation Dynamic Display) is a complete communication system that was developed by Australian Speech Pathologist Gayle Porter. The software needed to print out the various levels and types of PODD books is available to purchase in the UK from inclusive.co.uk. While there is always a need to personalise a communication book, a PODD book aims to provide the vast majority of the vocabulary you could need in a carefully structured format to encourage rich communication.

The PODD resources come with a detailed book that is full of ideas and information about supporting both PODD books and paper-based communication more generally. The Alternative Access templates also include video demonstrations. You can find videos of PODD in action on YouTube.
