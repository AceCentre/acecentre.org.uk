Symbol Charts

By their very nature, symbol charts tend to be highly personalised to the invidual. Therefore there are not many individual charts available to download. However, you will find the examples charts discussed in Chapter 4 on Ace Centre’s website here. There are also a few examples of symbol charts made to support other access methods if pointing is difficult.

On Ace Centre’s website, you will also find many different types of alphabet charts available for download here. As discussed, it is suggested that someone using symbols to communicate should also have access to an alphabet chart to support their development of literacy skills. You can find out more about using and adapting alphabet charts in Designing and Using Alphabet Charts – Getting started with AAC. This guide also shares information about other alphabet charts available for download or purchase, and how to go about making them.

Resource Making Software

The easiest way to make paper-based symbol resources is to use specially designed software. There are a number of different software products available in the UK. Some contain templates for developing charts that can be accessed in different ways.

Examples of specially designed chart making software in the UK includes:

• The Boardmaker range of software e.g. available from tobiidynavox.com

• Communicate in Print 3 e.g. available from widgit.com

• Matrix Maker Plus e.g. available from inclusive.co.uk

Alternatively, if you already have software for voice output communication or symbol recording (e.g. Grid 3 from thinksmartbox.com, MindExpress 5 from jabbla.co.uk, Communicator from tobiidynavox.com, Liberator’s PC Emulation software from liberator.co.uk, Symwriter from widgit.com, etc.), or are intending to purchase such software, you may be able to use this to develop and print charts.

Sometimes you can access resource making software through your local Speech and Language Therapy service or through a child’s educational environment. There are also a few counties that offer access to such software through their library service. You could contact your local library service to find out if this is available in your area. Some charities / nonprofit organisations also offer access to or funding for such software.

Commercially Available Resources to Assist with Access

Wedges that can be used to assist with positioning symbol charts are available from a number of suppliers including www.ability-world.com (switch and technology mounts) and www.inclusive.co.uk (wedge angled switch and tablet mounts)

You will find angled writing slopes and document holders that can beused to assist with positioning of symbol charts from a wide variety ofretailers including www.nrshealthcare.co.uk, www.posturite.co.uk and www.backinaction.co.uk

Dycem®, Tenura and other similar nonslip materials to place underneath symbol charts or books are available from a wide variety of retailers

You will find pointing tools sold by a variety of retailers including Dad in a Shed www.dadinashed.com

The Liberator E-tran frame can be purchased from suppliers including www.liberator.co.uk. It is a blank E-tran frame but is supplied with letter stickers.
