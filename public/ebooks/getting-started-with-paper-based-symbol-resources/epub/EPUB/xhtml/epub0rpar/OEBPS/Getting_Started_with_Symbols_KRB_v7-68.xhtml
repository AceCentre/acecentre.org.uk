Chapter 7 – Using Paper-based AAC if Pointing is Difficult

An E-tran frame (or eye-transfer frame) is simply a perspex rectangle with a central window. The frame is held between the individual and their communication partner and they make eye contact through the window in the centre of the frame. The person then looks at the symbol that they would like to select. As the frame is transparent, it makes it easier to follow where they are looking.

It is sometimes difficult to work out when the someone is looking around, and when they are purposefully eye pointing to a symbol. One strategy that can help is to encourage them to look back at you once they have made a selection. Angus demonstrates this in the next video.

Symbols do not just have to be presented on an E-tran frame. As in the video above, they can be cut up and placed in or on laminate pouches, producing lightweight eye pointing symbol charts. Glossy laminate pouches tend to work best as it is transparent after going through the laminator.
