Chapter 4 – Ideas and Resources for Getting Going

You can say all the things you could say with the simpler chart. But now there are a few extra symbols available. If they reach for the wand you could say that you can see “you want [the] bubbles”. If the bubble has landed, you could notice that it has “not pop”. You could notice that there are “lots [of] bubbles”. You could say “you catch [it]”. You could draw attention to bubbles “Wow! Look [at the] bubbles”. You could tell someone it’s time to “stop blow[ing] – I [need] more [mix]”. You could ask a question – “I have a question. [Do] you like [the] bubbles?”. You could offer to “help blow [the] bubbles”. You could talk about turns – “my [turn to] blow” or “your [turn]”. If you noticed the person is looking bored, you could say “[time to do something] different”.
