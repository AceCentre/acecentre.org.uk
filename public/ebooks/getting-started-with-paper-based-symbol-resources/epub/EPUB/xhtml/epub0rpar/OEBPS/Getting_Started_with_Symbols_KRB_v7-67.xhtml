Chapter 7 – Using Paper-based AAC if Pointing is Difficult

Tangible symbols such as those in the sand and water play example shown earlier, may also help access to symbol-based communication when there are complex visual issues.

If visual access to symbols is ultimately too difficult to be functional, an alternative access method may need to be considered. One option is auditory scanning (a form of listener mediated scanning) as used by Ruben in the video shown in Chapter 3. Essentially the communication partner speaks aloud the options and the person indicates when they hear the word or message they wish to communicate. However, some may still benefit from the visual input of symbols alongside the spoken word, perhaps using the “pick-up-and-show” approach described above or by simply pointing to the symbols.
