Foreword

The aim of this guide is explore access to paper-based AAC involving symbols for people who find it difficult to point due to physical and / or visual issues. It looks at each of the key access methods and explores how resources can be adjusted and adapted to meet individual needs.

Using Paper-Based Symbol Systems with Children - Getting started with AAC offers a more general introduction to developing, using and supporting paper-based AAC.

While this resource is all about access to symbol based resources, it is important to also provide individuals with an alphabet chart so they can explore and develop their use of letters. You can find lots more information about how to design, adapt, source and make alphabet charts in the guide, Designing and Using Alphabet Charts - Getting started with AAC.

Please note that this guide is not suggesting that paper-based AAC is better than electronic systems or that it must be used before an electronic communication aid is introduced. It is simply about valuing the role paper-based communication systems have to play, and sharing lots of ideas and strategies to help develop and support them. Many of the ideas and strategies within the guide may be useful when it comes to developing and supporting other forms of communication too.
