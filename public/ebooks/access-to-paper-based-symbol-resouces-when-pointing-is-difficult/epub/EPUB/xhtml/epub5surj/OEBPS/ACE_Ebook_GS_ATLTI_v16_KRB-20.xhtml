Use of Colour on a Page

Visual accessibility can be increased by using a high level of contrast between the background and foreground colours within a symbol cell and /or on the page itself. Commonly used high contrast combinations include yellow and black, and yellow and blue, although other options can be considered.

Background colour can also help someone to navigate through a busy communication book, as they can look out for the ‘blue chat page’, or the ‘orange people page’, for example.
