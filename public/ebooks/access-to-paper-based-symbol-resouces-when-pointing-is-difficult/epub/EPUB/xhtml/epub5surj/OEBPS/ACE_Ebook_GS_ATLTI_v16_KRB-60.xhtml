Chapter 10

﻿Making Paper-Based Symbol Charts

In this chapter:

• Consider how to make paper-based symbol charts

• Find out about commercially available products that can help

This chapter shares information about making and sourcing paper-based symbol charts.

When printing paper-based charts (or pages for a communication book), you might want to either laminate them or print them out on specialist paper. Laminating a chart makes it more robust and stops it getting tatty. For those pointing with a finger, it also makes the surface more slippery which can help with sliding your finger around. Alternatively, you could print a chart on so-called ‘indestructible’, tear-proof or waterproof printer paper.

When laminating, bear in mind that there are two types of laminate pouch available – gloss and matt. Gloss laminate tends to be the most common and widely used. However, it does reflect overhead lighting which can make it harder for the person using the chart and their communication partner to see what is being pointed to. Matt laminate tends to work a bit better from this perspective and is just as robust and slippery.

The only proviso here is that if you are creating an eye pointing chart in the style of an E-tran frame or an eyelink style chart, gloss laminate is best as this is transparent once it has been through the laminator whereas matt laminate is slightly cloudy.

To locate resources made in languages other than English, you may want to consult the websites of specialist AAC centres in relevant countries. The International Society for Augmentative and Alternative Communication (isaac-online.org) may be a good place to start. The website lists their chapters around the world, each of whom has contact details.

Symbol Charts

By their very nature, symbol charts tend to be highly personalised to the invidual. Therefore there are not many individual charts available to download. However, you will find some on the Ace Centre website - see acecentre.org.uk/product-category/symbol-charts. There are lots of symbol charts designed to be used with pointing. However, they could be adapted. There are also a few examples of symbol charts made to support other access methods.
