This can help those who have difficulties managing the visual complexity of a whole page of symbols, as they are only having to look at one symbol at a time. It can also help someone with visual field issues, as the symbol can be held in the place where it is easiest for the individual to see.

When modelling the use of symbols within a conversation or activity to help the individual learn both their meaning and how to use them, pick up and show enables the communication partner to present the symbol just where the individual can best see it. This is also a useful strategy when using symbols to support understanding.

For more information about modelling and other communication partner skills, see Chapter 8 of the resource Using Paper-Based Symbol Systems with Children - Getting started with AAC.
