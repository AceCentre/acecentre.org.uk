Chapter 6 - Coded Access

In this simplified example, the first chart contains the target symbols and the second chart is used by the individual to communicate the location of the target symbol. The colours are used to indicate the column or y-axis of the target symbol, and the numbers are used to indicate the row or x-axis. The second chart contains the relevant colours and numbers so the target symbol can be identified.

To communicate ‘fast’, the communicator would therefore select ‘green’ and ‘4’, as ‘fast’ is in the green column and the fourth row. Similarly, to communicate ‘uh oh’, they would select ‘blue’ and ‘three’ as ‘uh oh’ is in the blue column and the third row. This is demonstrated in the video below.
