Section 2 - Tools and Strategies to Support Pointing

Not everyone finds pointing tools comfortable to wear or hold, and some find it inconvenient to have another piece of equipment involved in communicating.

LED Light Pointer

Another tool that can be used to assist someone in pointing to a chart is an LED light pointer. These tend to be designed to help presenters highlight key points while delivering presentations. However, some people choose to use them to point to symbols on a chart.

One way of using an LED light pointer is to attach it to a pair of glasses. The person can then gently move their head to direct the light beam onto the symbols on a chart. However, the light pointer could be handheld, or attached to the body in another way.

Pointing Tools

Pointing tools are designed to help people with disabilities type or make onscreen selections more accurately, but they can also help some people point more accurately to a paper-based chart. They come in many different shapes and sizes. For some, simply wrapping an elastic band around the top of a ball point pen or stylus is enough of a help. Pointing tools are known by lots of names, including typing / keyboard aid, touch enabling device, dibber, universal cuff, accessible stylus and more.
