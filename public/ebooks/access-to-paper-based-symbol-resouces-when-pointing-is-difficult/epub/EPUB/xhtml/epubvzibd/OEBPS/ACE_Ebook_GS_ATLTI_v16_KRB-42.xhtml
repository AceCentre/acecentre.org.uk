﻿Communication Partner Skills for Encoding

As with all communication systems, the communication partner will need to model the encoding lots of times before there is any expectation of the individual making use of the strategy themselves.

See Getting Started with Paper-Based Symbol Resources – Getting Started with AAC for lots more information about communication partner skills.

The communication partner should also talk about what they are selecting and why. For example, “different has a blue border so I need to find the blue dot”. Modelling encoding can feel strange. However, it is important to demonstrate both how to use the vocabulary and how to select it. Modelling encoding will help someone learn how it all works.

In this video you will see Tamsin using her encoded communication book to share information. She moves around the pages in her book to collect the vocabulary she needs. She starts on her ‘top page’ and asks to go to her ‘family page’. She then goes back to her ‘top page’ and chooses to go to her ‘positions page’. Tamsin’s mother then models both how the encoding works and how to use the vocabulary to help Tamsin learn how to expand her story.

Tamsin using her encoded communication book

Practice really does help when it comes to modelling. If you can persevere with it, modelling will start to feel a bit more natural.
