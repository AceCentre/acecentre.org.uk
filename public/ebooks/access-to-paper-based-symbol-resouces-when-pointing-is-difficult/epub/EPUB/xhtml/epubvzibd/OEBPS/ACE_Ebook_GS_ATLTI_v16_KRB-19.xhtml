Chapter 3 - Visual Accessibility

Section 1 - Tools and Strategies to Support Visual Access

Colour or Black and White Symbols

With the exception of the Makaton® symbol library, which is exclusively black and white, most symbol libraries are drawn in full colour. However, some resource making software will let you convert coloured symbols into black and white. Whether you use colour or black and white versions really comes down to the needs of the individual.

Colour can help someone to identify the meaning of the symbol. In the example above, the fact that the juice in the glass and the circular fruit next to it are coloured orange make it more obvious that the symbol represents orange juice. Colour may also assist someone in locating a symbol on a busy chart as the use of colour provides more differentiation between each symbol.

However, for some, the colour in the symbol may be visually distracting, and the use of black and white may make the symbol appear cleaner and easier to see. For someone on the autistic spectrum with a strong preference for or dislike of a specific colour, then use of that colour in the symbol may even get in the way of them using the symbol communicatively.
