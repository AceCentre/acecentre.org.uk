﻿E-tran Frames and Alternatives

E-tran Frame

One way of presenting information when communicating through eye gaze is to use an E-tran frame. An E-tran (or eye transfer) frame is a Perspex® rectangle with a central cut out or window. The communication partner holds the frame between themselves and the person communicating with their eyes, making eye contact through the central window.

Being Perspex®, the frame is light and easy to position optimally wherever the person using it is located and however their head is positioned..

Symbols (or photographs, real objects or letters) are placed around the E-tran frame. These can be held in place by Blu-Tack or similar. The central window is designed to make it easier for the communication partner to see and follow the communicator’s eye movements.

Using double sided symbols can help the communication partner work out what the person is looking at. Alternatively, you can simply write the symbol name on the back of the symbol as this will be visible through the frame.
