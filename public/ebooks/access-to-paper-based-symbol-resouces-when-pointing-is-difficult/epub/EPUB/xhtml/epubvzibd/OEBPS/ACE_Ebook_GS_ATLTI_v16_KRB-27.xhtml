An E-tran frame can also be placed on a stand. As with presenting eye pointing charts on an easel file, this gives a ‘hands-free’ option. This can also be helpful for longer conversations as the communication partner’s arms can become tired.

Using a stand with an E-tran frame inevitably makes positoning it optimally for the individual a little more tricky. When the stand is on a non height adjustable table, it can sometimes result in the window of the frame being too low for eye contact to be made through it.

Chapter 4 - Eye Pointing

Section 1 - E-tran Frames and Alternatives

Using an E-tran frame with a stand

There are a number of commercially available blank E-tran frames (e.g. the Liberator E-Tran Frame and the Eye-Com™ Board). Alternatively, some Perspex® / acrylic suppliers offer a cut-to-size option.

The strength of an E-tran frame is that you can place symbols flexibly. However, this can also be a disadvantage, as symbols can end up being put in a different place on the frame each time it is used.

If a set of symbols are being presented regularly, it is worth deciding where on the frame you will place them and then noting this on the back of the symbol. This will mean the individual has to spend less time searching for the symbol they want as they will be familiar with its location. Motor planning is an important part of learning, and this is really helped by consistency in presentation.
