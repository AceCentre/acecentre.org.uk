Layouts

When placing symbols on an E-tran frame or eye pointing chart, people tend to arrange them in a landscape layout so the individual can make selections by looking to the four corners of the frame or chart. However, some people prefer to place symbols differently. By placing four symbols in a cross, selections can be made using up / down and left / right eye movements. Others may prefer more of a zig zag shape.

Presenting More Options – an introduction to encoding

The E-tran frames and eye pointing charts so far have shown just two to eight symbols at any one time. While this may be perfect for some, for others, this will be too limiting from a language perspective. Trying to communicate a more complex message would require many page changes or swapping out of symbols on a frame to give access to a larger vocabulary. One strategy that can be used to present more symbols at a time is encoding. Encoding enables you to select from more symbols on one chart while still using a limited number of different eye pointing movements.

Tamsin (shown in the photograph below chatting with her brother) is able select from thirty-six symbols at a time using just six different eye movements. This vastly increases the amount of language that is easily accessible to her, and speeds up communication by reducing the number of page turns involved in the construction of any one message.
