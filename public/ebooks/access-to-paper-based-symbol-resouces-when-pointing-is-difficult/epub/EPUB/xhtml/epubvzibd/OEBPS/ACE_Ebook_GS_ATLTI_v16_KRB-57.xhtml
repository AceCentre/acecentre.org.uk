• A brief introduction to combination access

Combination access is where someone uses more than one method to access a symbol chart or book.

It is possible to combine access methods within one communication system. For example, an individual might eye point or point with their hand to a group of symbols, and then use listener mediated scanning to select the specific symbol. Similarly, as discussed in Chapter 6, Coded Access, an individual might point directly to a group of symbols on a chart (using their body or eyes) and then use a code to identify the target symbol within that group (as in the example given).
