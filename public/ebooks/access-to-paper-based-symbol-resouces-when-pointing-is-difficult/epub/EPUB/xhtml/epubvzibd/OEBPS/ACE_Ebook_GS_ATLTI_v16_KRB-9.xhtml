Chapter 2 - Pointing

Section 1 - Chart Design to Support Pointing

The charts below illustrate the impact of changing the amount of space between the symbols. One has larger symbols and the other has slightly smaller symbols with more space between them. Having more space between the symbols can make it more obvious to the communication partner exactly which symbol is being pointed to. It can also make a chart feel less visually cluttered.

Using space to support pointing

﻿﻿Chart Design to Support Pointing

There are a lot of adjustments that can be made to a chart to help someone point to symbols more successfully. If you can, try and involve the person who will be using the chart in its design.

Use of Space

The use of space is one factor to consider in chart design. The picture below shows a child using three fingers to select a symbol. The chart they are using is working well, and it is clear in the picture that the child is selecting the symbol ‘great’. However, if the symbols were closer together, they might also be inadvertently selecting the ‘you, your’ symbol making their communication less clear.
