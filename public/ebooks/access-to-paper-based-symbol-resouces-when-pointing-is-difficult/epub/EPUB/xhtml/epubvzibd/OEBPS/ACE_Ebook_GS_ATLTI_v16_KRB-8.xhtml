Chapter 2

﻿Pointing

In this chapter:

• Consider factors that might influence the design of a communication chart or book to facilitate pointing

• Find out about tools and strategies to support pointing

• Briefly consider the exchange method

Pointing to symbols with a finger, hand, elbow, or some other part of the body is also known as direct access, direct selection or direct touch. You can never generalise in the field of AAC, but if someone can point to a symbol chart directly, this tends to be their preferred way of selecting symbols.

This chapter looks at how charts can be designed to make them easier for an individual to point to. Note that when designing a chart for an individual, you may also want to take into account some of factors discussed in Chapter 3, Visual Accessibility.

This chapter also takes a look at tools and strategies that can help someone point more successfully.

FInally it considers the exchange method, which is a slightly different form of direct access to symbols..
