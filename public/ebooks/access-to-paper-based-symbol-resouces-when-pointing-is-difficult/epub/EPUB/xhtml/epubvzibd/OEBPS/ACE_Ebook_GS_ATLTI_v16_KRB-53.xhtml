A great way to get started with a more systematic approach to listener mediated scanning is to keep a notebook and pen (or smartphone) to hand, and start writing down the options required in different situations. As with all AAC, try to keep things fun, particularly in the beginning. Listener mediated scanning is hard work. Getting started needs to be fun, rewarding and highly motivating.

Special Considerations for Auditory Approaches

When using auditory or auditory and visual listener mediated scanning you can simply speak aloud the symbol labels. However, you may want to use a more phrase based approach.

The same strategy of writing down the options required in different situations is a great way of getting started. However, try and avoid writing down the questions you find yourself asking the individual. Instead, write from the perspective of the individual themselves: For example, rather than having an option of ‘Do you want a drink?’, try writing ‘I want a drink’. It means that instead of answering a question, the individual is telling you something.

You might want to start by choosing an activity, and thinking about the different options available. For example,

Toys – I want…

• Vibrating cushion

• Bells

• Squishy ball

• A different toy [useful as you may not have all the options on the list and this gives the individual a way of asking to ask you to offer some different options]

• Stop playing with toys

You could also add some comments about the toys to the list like, “This is fun”.

Such lists don’t just have to be about choice making though. You could think of an activity and write down the ways an individual could engage with it.

For example, playing with play dough, you could have the options:

I want to...

• Roll the play dough

• Use an animal cutter

• Make an ice cream

• Make a pizza

• Do something different

• Stop playing with play dough

Again, you could add comments to the list like, “It’s sticky”, “Funny smell”, “I like this”, “Please help”, or “Let’s start again”.
