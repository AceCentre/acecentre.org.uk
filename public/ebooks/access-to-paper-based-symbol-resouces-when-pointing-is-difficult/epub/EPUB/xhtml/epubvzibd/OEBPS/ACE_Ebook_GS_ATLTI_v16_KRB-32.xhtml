Presenting More Options â€“ EyeLink

Another way of presenting more options for eye pointing is to use an EyeLink approach. This approach tends to be based around letters. There is no reason why symbols could not be presented in this way. However, it would probably only be worth exploring this with a confident eye pointer.

When using an eye pointing chart or an E-tran frame, the chart or frame is held in one position and the person moves their eyes to point at their chosen symbol. With an EyeLink approach however, the transparent sheet is constantly being moved. The communication partner watches the eye movement of the individual and moves the sheet in the direction the individual is eye pointing to so that they end up both looking at the same thing.
