﻿Organising Information

The simplest way of scanning through options is to offer each symbol in turn. However, this can be time consuming, particularly when you are offering lots of options. To speed up the process you may want to divide the information into groups.

This communication chart has been grouped by highlighting rows of symbols. The idea is that you offer each row in turn before offering the individual options along the chosen row:

A communication chart where symbols are grouped by row www.acecentre.org.uk

By grouping the symbols on this chart, you can communicate options along the bottom row much more quickly than if you simply offered each symbol in turn. Without grouping, you would need to scan through 19 symbols to get to the symbol ‘slow’. By grouping, you first scan through 4 rows, and then 4 symbols to get to the symbol ‘slow’. This is a total of 8 options which is much less than the 19 if there was no grouping. Grouping really does increase the speed with which symbols can be selected, and therefore the pace of the conversation.
