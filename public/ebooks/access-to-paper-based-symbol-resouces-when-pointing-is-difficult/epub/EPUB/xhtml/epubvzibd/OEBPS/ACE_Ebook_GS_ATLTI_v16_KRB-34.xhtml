﻿Communication Partner Skills for Eye Pointing

There are some useful skills that a communication partner can employ to support a person who is using eye pointing to communicate.

Giving Feedback

Especially when someone is getting started with eye pointing, it can be helpful to give feedback on where you think they are looking as you respond.

Try and feedback using a statement rather than a question. For example, you could simply say “like” while tapping the like symbol, or “You are saying ‘like’”) rather than asking “Are you looking at ‘like’?” The individual can still respond to the statement and make it clear that you are wrong. However, using a statement rather than a question takes away the feeling of someone double checking or questioning every utterance you make.

Chatting not Testing

There is something about communicating using eye pointing that can lead to feelings of anxiety in the communication partner. They will often check, double check and even triple check that they have correctly understood the selection. The problem with this is that it slows communication down and can lead to frustration. Can you imagine how irritating you would find it if someone asked if they had understood you correctly multiple times after every single sentence you uttered?! Many of us would quickly give up speaking under those circumstances, and the same is true of communicating through eye pointing. Try and lift the pressure a little.
