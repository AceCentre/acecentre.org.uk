Section 1 - Chart Design to Support Pointing

Pick Up and Show

Another way of designing a chart a little differently to help someone with their access is to use Pick up and Show.

Pick up and show involves someone picking up a symbol and showing it to their communication partner. Just occasionally, someone may find it easier to grab a symbol that is Velcroed™ to a page and pull it off than they do to point to a specific symbol on a page. Pick up and show materials can look similar to PECS® materials, but they are used quite differently in that there is no requirement to exchange the symbol. The individual merely shows the symbol to the communication partner.

To keep track of symbols, and to assist with motor learning, it is helpful to always put the symbols back in the same place on the chart. The easiest way to achieve this is to print two copies of the communication chart. One copy of the chart is laminated. The other copy is cut up into the individual symbols and each symbol is laminated. The individual symbols are then Velcroed™ onto their corresponding symbol on the full laminated chart. That way, it is simply a case of matching up the symbols when returning them to the chart.
