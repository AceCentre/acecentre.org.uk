Chapter 11 - Next Steps - Developing a Communication Book

PODD (Pragmatic Organisation Dynamic Display) is a complete communication system that was developed by Australian Speech Pathologist, Gayle Porter. The software needed to print out the various levels and types of PODD books is available to purchase in the UK from inclusive.co.uk.

While there is always a need to personalise a communication book, a PODD book aims to provide the vast majority of the vocabulary you could need in a carefully structured format to encourage rich communication.

The PODD resources comes with a detailed book that is full of ideas and information about supporting both PODD books and paper-based communication more generally. The Alternative Access templates also include video demonstrations. You can find videos of PODD in action on YouTube.

The Direct Access PODD templates are designed for direct pointing with a whole hand, finger or tool. Some of the templates can be modified to accommodate pick up and show or listener mediated scanning access methods (using column row scanning).

PODD Alternative Access templates meet a wider range of access needs. The resources contains 22 different Alternative Access PODD communication books at different levels of language complexity. Access methods supported include: eye pointing, listener mediated scanning, a variety of combination access options and coded access. Video demonstrations of each book are provided alongside detailed information about their construction and use.

Courses on PODD are available in the UK, and are a great way of learning more about this way of communicating. You can find a list of UK-based trainers and information about forthcoming courses at podd.dk (click on the Union Jack flag). The templates themselves can be purchased from inclusive.co.uk.
