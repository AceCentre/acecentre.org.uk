Positioning the Chart or Book

As well as impacting upon the design of the chart as discussed in the previous section, understanding how someone moves may also affect the placement of a chart or book in relation to the individual. Again, the aim is to make it as easy as possible to select symbols. A minor adjustment to positioning can make quite a big difference for some.

A very simple example would be if someone uses their right hand to select symbols, you might want to place the chart or book on the right hand side of a table or tray so that movement of their right hand is as easy and comfortable as possible. This could then be documented in a Communication Passport.
