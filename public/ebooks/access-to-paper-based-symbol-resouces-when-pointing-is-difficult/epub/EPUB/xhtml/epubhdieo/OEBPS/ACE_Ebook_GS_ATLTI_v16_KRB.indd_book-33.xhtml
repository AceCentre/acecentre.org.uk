Section 3 - Making a Selection Using Eye Pointing

ï»¿Making a Selection Using Eye Pointing

One of the challenges of using eye pointing is differentiating between a searching look and a choosing look. In other words, how does the communication partner know whether someone is just searching for the symbol they want, or actually looking at the option they want to communicate.

Some are able to indicate the symbol they have chosen by just resting their eyes on that symbol for fractionally longer. For others, particularly in the early stages of eye pointing, this may not be very clear.

One strategy that can be tried is look-back-at-me. The communication partner encourages the communicator to look around at the options, then look purposefully at the symbol they want to communicate (briefly holding their gaze on it). They then look back at the communication partner to confirm that this is the symbol they are communicating.
