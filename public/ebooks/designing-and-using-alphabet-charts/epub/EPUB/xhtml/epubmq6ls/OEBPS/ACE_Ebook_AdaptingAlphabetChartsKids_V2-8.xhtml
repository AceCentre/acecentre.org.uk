﻿Tiago is eight years old. He has cerebral palsy and finds it difficult to use his hands. He can communicate using speech with people who know him very well, although sometimes needs a back up option if they can’t understand what he is saying. These tools become essential when communicating with someone who doesn’t know him so well.

Tiago has an alphabet chart that has been specially adapted for use with eye movements. Copies are all round his house so that one can be reached quickly in the event of a problem. There are copies tucked down the side of the sofa, in the bathroom, in the kitchen, in his bedroom - all over the place! There’s also a copy of the alphabet chart permanently attached to his wheelchair. Being made of laminate, the charts are light and fairly robust, and are easy to replace if they do get damaged.

The letters on Tiago’s chart are arranged in an encoded layout. To communicate with the chart, Tiago first looks to the group that contains his target letter. He then clarifies which letter he wants by looking at the coloured square that matches the colour of his target letter.

Tiago also has a sophisticated computer to support his communication that he controls using eye movements. He is a great reader and speller, and can use an onscreen keyboard to spell out and then speak whatever he wants to say. However, there are times when a simple chart is quicker and easier to use.

For example, Tiago was travelling in the back of the car with his step-sister, and she just couldn’t work out what he was saying. Frustration all round! The computer was in the boot, but couldn’t be used in the back seat of the car anyway. Luckily, Mum was in the front seat and had an alphabet chart to hand - she passed it back to them and the problem was quickly resolved.
