﻿Selecting Letters When Eye Pointing

One of the challenges of using eye pointing with an E-tran frame or chart can be differentiating between a searching look and a selecting look. In other words, how does the communication partner know whether the individual is searching for the letter or actually looking at their desired letter? The easiest way of signalling that something is being selected is for the individual to hold their gaze on it for a short period of time or until the communication partner responds.

This video shows more of Tiago and Katharine chatting using his encoded eye pointing chart. Tiago’s eye pointing is clear to read. He simply holds his gaze fractionally longer on the desired group of letters (referred to as blocks in the video) or colour to indicate that he is making a selection.
