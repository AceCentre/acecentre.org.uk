The MegabeeThe Megabee is effectively an electronic E-tran fram. It incorporates a display which allows the communication partner to keep track of what has been selected without using pen and paper / similar. The display can be read by both the communication partner and the person communicating using eye pointing. However, there is no speech output.

You can read more about the Megabee at megabee.net.

To use the Megabee, the person first looks to the coloured block that contains their target letter. The communication partner presses the corresponding coloured button with their thumb. The person then looks to the coloured block that matches the colour of their target letter. The communication partner presses this coloured button, and the letter or number that is indicated by these two colours is displayed on the small screen for both to see.

Lorraine uses a Megabee as well as an eye control computer. In this video, she is spelling out three letters on the Megabee and Charlie explains how he is recording her selections. For the letter ‘w’, she looks at the red block and then the black block as ‘w’ is situated in the red block and is itself coloured black. For the letter ‘e’ she looks at the blue block and then the black block; and finally for the letter ‘n’ she looks at the green block and then the yellow block. Spelling ‘when’ as ‘wen’ is a deliberate strategy Lorraine uses to speed up communication. She often simplifies words where possible.

This side is seen by the communication partner. The colourful buttons on the left- and right-hand sides above the handles are used to record the letters selected. This means the communication partner does not have to use pen and paper to keep track of the conversation.
