﻿Using a KeyguardA keyguard is a cover that fits over a keyboard or computer screen and contains holes through which keys or areas of the screen can be selected. It allows an individual to rest their hand on the cover and make selections through the holes.

Some people find that using a keyguard with their alphabet chart makes it easier for their communication partner to work out which letter is being pointed to. They can be particularly beneficial if the person experiences tremor when pointing. FAB™ alphabet charts incorporate a keyguard into their design – see fab.uk.com. and come in a variety of layouts and sizes. There are also made in a variety of languages. The Liberator letter board (liberator.co.uk) offers another keyguard option.
