﻿﻿Listener Mediated Scanning Without a Chart (Auditory Scanning)

For those with significant visual difficulties, there is no need to rely on a printed chart. The alphabet can simply be spoken aloud, with the individual indicating ‘yes’ when they hear their target letter. Even when there are no visual issues, the individual and their communication partner may find they prefer to manage without a printed chart. This can also be a quick way of communicating in the dark, in a vehicle or in water.

Listener mediated scanning without a chart is also known as auditory scanning, and is demonstrated in the video below. In this example, the alphabet has been divided into two groups – ‘A to M’ and ‘N to Z’. The communication partner first offers ‘A’ or ‘N’, then goes on to speak aloud the letters that follow either ‘A’ or ‘N’ depending upon the choice made. The alphabet doesn’t have to be split in half - it could be grouped by vowels, or by any other means that makes sense to the individual.
