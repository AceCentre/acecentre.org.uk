﻿The Vocal Eyes Alphabet Chart

Jason Becker, a musician, uses a specially designed encoded eye pointing chart to support his communication. His alphabet chart is similar to the examples above, but uses position to identify the letters. The chart is free to download from jasonbeckerarchive.com/eye_communication.html.

An encoded eye pointing chart does not have to involve colour. Encoded layouts can be set up in lots of different ways including using shading, shapes, numbers and so on.

For example, this is an eye pointing chart that has been encoded using numbers. It is available to download from acecentre.org.uk. To select the letter ‘B’, the individual would first eye point to the group of letters in the top left corner that contains ‘A, B, C, D, E, F’. To clarify that ‘B’ was their desired letter, they would then eye point to the large number ‘2’ in the top centre, because ‘B’ is followed by the small number ‘2’.

Jason first looks at the group that contains his target letter. He then uses up, down, left or right eye movement to clarify which of the four letters in that group he is selecting.

You can see a video of him using his chart on the download page above.
