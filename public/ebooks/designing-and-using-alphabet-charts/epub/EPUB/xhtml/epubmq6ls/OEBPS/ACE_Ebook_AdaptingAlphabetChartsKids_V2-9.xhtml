Michael has Motor Neuron Disease and has begun to find it difficult to talk. He is getting going with using paper-based and electronic aids to support his communication.

Michael was recently introduced to an alphabet chart that he accesses using listener mediated scanning. The communication partner reads aloud and points to the letters of the alphabet. When the communication partner reaches his target letter, Michael looks at them to indicate that that is the letter he wishes to select.

To increase the efficiency of this communication tool, the alphabet on his chart is organised so that letters that are frequently used in English such as ‘e’ and ‘t’ are offered before letters like ‘q’ and ‘z’ which don’t get used so often. This is known as a frequency layout.

Michael is also learning to use a tablet device that he accesses with a switch.
