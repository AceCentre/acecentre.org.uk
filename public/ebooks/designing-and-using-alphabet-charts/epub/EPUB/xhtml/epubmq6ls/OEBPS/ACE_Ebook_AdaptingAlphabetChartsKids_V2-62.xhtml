﻿Communication Books

A communication book is a place where you can gather together a much larger number of useful words and phrases. It sometimes evolves from a selection of communication charts. The index page of a communication book could also be used for topic cueing.

A communication book can be separate from an alphabet chart. If someone is using eye pointing or listener mediated scanning, it may be helpful to have a message on the alphabet chart signalling that they want to use their communication book, and a corresponding message within the book to indicate that they want to use their alphabet chart. Alternatively, as in most of the examples below, you could bring the two together so that the alphabet chart is part of the communication book.

For a communication book to be useful, the phrases will need to be categorised in some way. Although the phrases could be presented in alphabetical order, it is often easier to identify category headings such as Chat, People, Care, Health issues, Feelings, Time / Events, Pets, etc. The categories chosen will be dependent upon the individual, their lifestyle and their communication needs. The categories needed, and their content, may well change over time.

A communication book can be produced in all shapes and sizes, and a lot of the issues regarding font size, layout and presentation that are discussed in Chapter 9 in relation to alphabet charts, are also relevant to the production of a communication book. Ace Centre (and others) have produced several freely downloadable templates (see Chapter 13), but you may choose to make your own.

As with alphabet charts, communication books can be set up to be accessed in different ways:

• By pointing with a finger or other body part• Using eye movement• Using listener mediated scanning• Using a combination of methods

Communication Books that can be Accessed by PointingCommunication books accessed by pointing can be created in lots of different ways. For example, they can be handwritten messages in a small notebook, printed pages within an A4 or A5 file or made using a downloadable template (see Chapter 13 for more information).
