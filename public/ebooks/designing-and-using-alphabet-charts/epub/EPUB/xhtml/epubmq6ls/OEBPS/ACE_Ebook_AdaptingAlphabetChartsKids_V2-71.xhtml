If the communication partner can’t get to the bottom of the problem, or work out what the person is trying to communicate, it is usually best to be open about the situation rather than pretending it hasn’t happened. The communication partner could offer to come back to it another time. Asking questions about the context can sometimes help e.g. Who are we talking about? What situation are w talking about? Is it something that has already happened / etc. Cueing charts can also help in such situations.

﻿We all make mistakes - keep calm and carry on

One thing to bear in mind if a communication partner is going to attempt to predict is that they should confirm they have guessed correctly before moving on. It may also be worth agreeing a maximum number of guesses a communication partner will make before the person returns to spelling out the word or sentence.

Acknowledge When Things Go WrongCommunication will sometimes go wrong - It does even when both parties are communicating using speech. It is helpful if communication partners acknowledge when things go wrong and take steps to sort it out.
