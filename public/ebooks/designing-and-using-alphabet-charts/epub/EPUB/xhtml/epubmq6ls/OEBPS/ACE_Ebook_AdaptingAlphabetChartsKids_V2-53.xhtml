A demonstration of coded access to an alphabet chart

Coded access might be considered if someone finds it hard to refine their pointing sufficiently to point to twenty-six separate letters or if they have a limited range of movement.

Coded charts can be set up and accessed in lots of different ways. As well as pointing directly to the codes, the codes could be presented on an eye pointing chart or accessed using listener mediated scanning.

In Summary - Coded Layouts

Why consider a coded layout?

Coded layouts reduce the number of target areas someone must select from to access the alphabet.

What access methods does it suit?

Coded layouts may be used to support peple who find it difficult to point to letters either due to issues around accuracy or due to having a limited range of movement. It is possible to use a coded layout with all access methods.

What are the disadvantages?

There are two charts to position and keep track of.

Each letter requires at least two movements to communicate its location.

Coded access can feel confusing initially.
