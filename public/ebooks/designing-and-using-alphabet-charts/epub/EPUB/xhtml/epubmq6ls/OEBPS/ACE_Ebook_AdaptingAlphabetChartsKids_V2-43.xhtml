A standard computer keyboard has letters laid out in a QWERTY format, and this can be replicated on an alphabet chart. However, there are other ways of laying out letters. This chapter explores five different approaches.

There are advantages and disadvantages to each option, and some suit different access methods better than others. At the end of the day, the choice will come down to personal preference and what seems to work best for an individual and their communication partner(s).

Something to bear in mind is how an individual uses the alphabet when writing and accessing a computer. Consistency across different tools can be helpful. However, there are sometimes good reasons for having different keyboard layouts for different tools.

Ideally, the individual should be given the opportunity to try some different options and see what feels right for them.
