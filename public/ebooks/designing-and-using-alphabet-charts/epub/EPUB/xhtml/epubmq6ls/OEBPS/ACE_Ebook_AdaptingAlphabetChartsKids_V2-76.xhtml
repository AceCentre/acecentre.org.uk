This chapter shares information about sourcing alphabet charts and communication books (both free of charge and for purchase), and looks at software that can help you make your own paper-based resources. Although we have tried to include as many resources as we can, the lists of what’s available are not comprehensive.

With resources that you download and print at home, you might want to either ﻿laminate them or print them out on specialist paper. Laminating an alphabet chart or communication book makes it more robust and stops it getting tatty. For those pointing with a finger, it also makes the surface more slippery which can help with sliding a finger around. Alternatively, you could print a chart on so-called ‘indestructible’, tear-proof or waterproof printer paper.

When laminating, bear in mind that there are two types of laminate pouch available – gloss and matt. Gloss laminate tends to be the most common and widely used. However, it does reflect overhead lighting which can make it harder for everyone to see the letters. Matt laminate tends to work a bit better from this perspective and is just as robust and slippery.

The only proviso here is that if you are creating an eye pointing chart in the style of an E-tran frame or an eyelink style chart, gloss laminate is best as this is transparent once it has been through the laminator. Matt laminate is slightly cloudy so is less suitable for this purpose.
