To locate resources made in languages other than English, you may want to consult the websites of specialist AAC centres in relevant countries. The International Society for Augmentative and Alternative Communication (isaac-online.org) may be a good place to start. The website lists their chapters around the world, each of whom has contact details. The Speakbook is available in a growing number of languages and contains alphabet charts for both pointing and eye pointing.

Alphabet Charts and Related Equipment - Resources available for purchase

The Stroke Association sells an alphabet chart pack that has been designed to help people with aphasia following a stroke who are able to spell. It includes both alphabetical and qwerty charts, numbers, days and months, and a clock with moveable hands.
