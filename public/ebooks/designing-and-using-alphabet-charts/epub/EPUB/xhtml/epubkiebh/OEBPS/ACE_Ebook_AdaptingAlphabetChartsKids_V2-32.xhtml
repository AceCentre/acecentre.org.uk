The look of the chart can be adjusted and personalised to make it work better for the individual and their communication partner(s) (the font size might be increased or the colours adjusted, for example) - see Chapter 9 for more information. Words and phrases that are regularly used may be accessed more efficiently using some of the strategies discussed in Chapter 10. As discussed in Chapter 11, some individuals may find it speeds things up if they keep their sentences concise or even just spell out key words, but this is a personal decision.

How it Works

The simplest way of scanning through the alphabet is for the communication partner to offer each letter in turn in alphabetical order as in the video above.

However, while straightforward, pointing to each letter in turn is not necessarily the quickest way of delivering the alphabet. To select the letter ‘T’ for example, the individual must first wait while their communication partner points to and / or speaks aloud the nineteen letters that precede it.

To speed up the process, the letters can be grouped together. The communication partner first scans through the groups. The individual chooses the group containing their letter, then the communication partner offers each letter within that group.

﻿This letters in this ABC alphabet chart have been grouped in rows. Each row begins with a ﻿vowel. This breaks the alphabet up into five groups that are identified by the name of the vowel at the start of each group / row.
