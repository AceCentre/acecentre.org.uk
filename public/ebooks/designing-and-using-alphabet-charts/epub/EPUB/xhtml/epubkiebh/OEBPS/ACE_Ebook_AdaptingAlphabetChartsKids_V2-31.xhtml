The tools and strategies discussed up until now all involve directly selecting a letter, whether by pointing with a finger (or other body part), a pointing tool or pointing with eye movement. However, an alternative approach is for the communication partner to offer the alphabet by pointing letter-by-letter and / or speaking the alphabet aloud. The individual communicates ‘yes’ when the communication partner points to and / or says their target letter.

This approach is known as listener mediated scanning or partner assisted scanning. In other words, the listener (communication partner) scans through the alphabet for the individual. Michael and Ethan, introduced in Chapter 1, both use listener mediated scanning in different ways.

To use listener mediated scanning, the individual needs to be able to indicate ‘yes’ in some way. This can be done by any agreed signal – a vocalisation, a movement with part of the body like a hand, head or eye movement, or some other means. If it is a method that people who don’t know them might not immediately recognise or understand to be ‘yes’, it may be worth documenting it to ensure that everyone is responding correctly.
