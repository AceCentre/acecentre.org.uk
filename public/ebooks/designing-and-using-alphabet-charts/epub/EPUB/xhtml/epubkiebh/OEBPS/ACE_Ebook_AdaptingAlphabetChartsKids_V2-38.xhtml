When speaking aloud the letters, whether auditory scanning or alongside pointing to the letters on a chart, the communication partner should aim to deliver the options using a fairly neutral voice. Expression should be kept to a minimum, and the volume should be as low as is appropriate to the individual and the environment. When the individual has spelled out a word, the communication partner can speak that word aloud in a more natural / social voice. The difference in tone can be heard in the video above. This approach provides a clear differentiation between the tool and what is being communicated.

It can sometimes be hard for a communication partner to juggle looking at the letters on the chart and watching the individual for a yes response, particularly if the ‘yes’ does not involve a sound. This does tend to get easier. As the communication partners become more familiar with the letters on the chart, they can give the individual more of their visual attention.

Communication partners should check whether the individual has more to say before putting the chart down so that they are not inadvertently cutting the individual off mid conversation.
