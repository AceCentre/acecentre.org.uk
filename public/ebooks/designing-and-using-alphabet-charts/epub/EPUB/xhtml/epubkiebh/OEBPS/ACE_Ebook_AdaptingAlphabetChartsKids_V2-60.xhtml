﻿﻿﻿Incorporating Core Words

When we communicate, there is a core set of words that we use time and time again. There are various lists of such commonly used or high frequency words in english, e.g. here on Wikipedia. While such lists are useful for reference, their relevance will vary from person to person and they will always benefit from some personalising.

Including some of these core words on the alphabet chart itself, or in a communication chart or book can speed communication. The A4 communication book shared later in this chapter illustrates this approach.

These alphabet charts all incorporate such core words. They are designed to be accessed directly by pointing, but modified versions could be used with listener mediated scanning. They reduce the number of words that need to be spelled out, but make the chart larger and busier to look at:

﻿Speech and Language Therapist ﻿Susan Stayte developed an eye pointing chart involving lots of core words alongside the alphabet for a young man with excellent eye pointing skills who wanted to communicate quickly and efficiently.
