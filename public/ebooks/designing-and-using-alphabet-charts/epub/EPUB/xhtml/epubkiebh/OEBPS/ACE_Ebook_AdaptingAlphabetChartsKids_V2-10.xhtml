﻿Ethan is twelve years old and attends a special school. He uses a large symbol-based communication book and loves to share a good joke! Ethan also has an alphabet chart that he is using to explore spelling words.

Ethan looks up to communicate ‘yes’, and looks right to communicate ‘no’. He accesses his symbol-based communication book using listener mediated scanning. This means that his communication partner offers him options, and Ethan communicates ‘yes’ by looking up when he sees and hears the one he wants.

Ethan is exploring voice output communication options that he might access using eye gaze or a switch. At the moment though, his communication book is working best for him.

You can see Ethan working on spelling his name using his alphabet chart in the video. As with his communication book, he accesses the chart using listener mediated scanning. Ethan makes a great start at picking out some of the letters. Alli then provides a little more support to enable him to write his name accurately. This is a valuable early literacy experience.

The alphabet chart is effectively acting as an alternative pencil. In time, it is hoped that Ethan’s computer access skills will develop so that he can also work on literacy activities on a computer. However, for now, he really benefits from the support and flexibility spelling with a communication partner brings.

As discussed later on in this guide, Ethan also benefits from the fact that the alphabet is broken down across a series of pages, so he only has to choose from a few letters at any one time. As he gains experience and confidence with this chart, he may be able to move on to selecting letters from a full alphabet chart.
