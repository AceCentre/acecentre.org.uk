This alphabet chart has been customised with a few useful phrases. ‘Delete’ and ‘start again’ are there to deal with communication issues and a ‘space’ option is included. It also has some basic punctuation and numbers. Available to download from acecentre.org.uk

 

Sometimes people choose to include many more words and phrases on their alphabet chart which is explored in the section on incorporating core words in Chapter 10.

Messages to flag up and deal with communication problems like ‘mistake’, ‘delete’ or ‘start over’ can be useful to have on a chart, as can a ‘space’ or ‘next word’ option.

Punctuation can be included. A question mark and full stop are often sufficient although some like to include other punctuation such as an exclamation mark and a comma.

Including numbers can be helpful as it saves spelling them out, although they do take up space. The chart above includes numerals with a percentage sign and a colon to help with indicating time. A currency sign could also be useful. An alternative approach is to print numbers on a separate chart or on the reverse of the alphabet chart.

Personalising a Chart

Alphabet charts don’t have to be plain! As discussed above, the colours can be varied to make them easier to see. They can also be printed with a coloured background, or on coloured paper just because someone likes the colour! You might want to use a wallpaper style background with the logo of a favourite sports team, or similar. Some also choose to personalise charts with family pictures or pictures of pets.

Personalising an alphabet chart can increase motivation to use it. It can also provide a great talking point!

An alternative to braille is Moon. Moon is a tactile alphabet that has sometimes been used by people who have lost sight later in life or whose lack of sensitivity means they struggle to perceive the difference between the braille letters. See the RNIB website here for more information. Note that the RNIB does not actively support Moon and has concerns about its viability.
