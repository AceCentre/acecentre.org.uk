﻿Eye Pointing Directly to Letters – ‘EyeLink’

As discussed above, eye pointing to letters is most often done on an encoded E-tran frame or eye pointing chart. EyeLink is an alternative way of eye pointing to letters that doesn’t involve encoding. As you will see, letters are selected quite differently.

These photographs show Lisa’s customised EyeLink communication chart. It has been made by printing and cutting out individual letters and then laminating them to create the chart. An Eyelink chart can also be created by printing the alphabet on a piece of acetate.

The EyeLink chart pictured above belongs to Lisa, who is a choreographer. The video below shows her using her chart and explaining how it works. Note that Lisa chooses to look at the mirror image of the alphabet so that her communication partners can see it in alphabetical order. It is all down to personal preference.

As with all such tools, training and time for practise is important. For information about making or sourcing an EyeLink chart, see Chapter 13.

 

How to use the EyeLink Communication Method

 

The transparent EyeLink chart is held by the communication partner and eye contact is made through it. The individual then looks at the letter they wish to select. The communication partner moves the chart in response to where the individual is looking, until both end up looking straight ahead at the same letter. The communication partner speaks aloud the letter and the individual can indicate if a mistake has been made.

Typically, the person using the chart to communicate sees the ‘correct’ layout (i.e. the letters in alphabetical order) and the communication partner sees the mirror image.
