﻿Getting Started with Eye Pointing and Encoding

Learning to make a selection using eye pointing involves lots of different skills and is not necessarily something that everyone wil be able to do immediately, and it is not an access method that is suitable for everyone. The Vision and Communication Research Team at UCL and Great Ormond Street Hospital are active in this area - see ucl.ac.uk/gaze. Amongst other work, they have developed an eye pointing classification scale to describe and support the use of looking in communication by children with complex physical disabilities. This can be downloaded from their website.

In order to build skills in eye pointing, one approach is to start by using it within fun games or activities such as ‘I spy’ or hangman. The communication partner responds to where they are looking, but mistakes are fun rather than the cause of communication breakdown.

Encoding is also not necessarily straightforward in the beginning. To help someone get started with encoding, the steps could be broken down as with the EyeTalk. The EyeTalk itself could be used for this, or an EyeTalk-style book created using your choice of encoded alphabet chart.

﻿Another approach to getting started with encoding is to ask the individual to look at the group containing their letter, but then use listener mediated scanning to support them to select the letter from within that group.
