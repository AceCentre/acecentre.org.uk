﻿﻿Beatrice’s Combination Access Communication ToolBeatrice has motor neuron disease and, alongside an electronic communication aid, uses a bespoke communication system based on combination access. Again, it involves both eye pointing and listener mediated scanning. However, it is based around a white board and takes a slightly different approach to those discussed so far.

The AE-EYE-OU communication method, developed by Amy Roman, is another slightly different example of a system that uses a combination of eye pointing and listener mediated scanning. This video by Caroline Panlilio illustrates its use:

The approach to getting started with encoding described above where the person eye points to the group containing their letter and then selects their letter using listener mediated scanning is another example of combination access.

Janet holds the whiteboard and watches to see which group of letters Beatrice points to with her eyes. Janet will then use listener mediated scanning to offer the letters within the chosen group.

Janet has written what Beatrice has communicated in blue ink. Reflecting on the use of the whiteboard in comparison with her electronic communication aid, Beatrice observes, “It’s quicker when you are half way through dinner and decide you want more salt”
