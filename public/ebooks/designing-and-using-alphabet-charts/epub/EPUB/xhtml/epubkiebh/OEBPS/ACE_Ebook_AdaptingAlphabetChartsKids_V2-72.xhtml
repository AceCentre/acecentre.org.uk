﻿Avoid the Trap of Endless Double CheckingIf a communication partner is struggling or feeling anxious about communicating in a new way, they may start routinely double checking everything being communicated, seeking reassurance that they have correctly understood after each letter or word. While it is good that someone doesn’t try and pretend they have understood when they haven’t, being asked to reconfirm every single letter or word communicated can quickly lead to frustration and really slow things down.

It may work better if the communication partner simply repeats back or writes down what they think has been communicated. The person using the chart can then indicate to them if they have got it wrong, but if it’s right, conversation just keeps moving along.

Check Whether or Not the Person Has More to SayIf the alphabet chart is held and / or delivered by the communication partner (some eye pointing systems, listener mediated scanning or combination access) then the communication partner should check whether the person has more to say after delivering a message. It can be frustrating if the communication partner put the tool down thinking the conversation is over when in fact they have something else to say. It is like being cut off mid conversation.

It would have been easy for Katherine to have stopped after the first word but instead she checked whether he had more to say, and indeed, he did!

Don’t Speak for Someone (unless you’ve been asked to)Having someone speak on their behalf must be the individual’s decision. In a busy conversation, they might look to a familiar communication partner to answer for them. However, it is important that no communication partner assumes that they should speak for someone. They should always check that the person is happy for them to speak on their behalf before they do so.

Someone might find it helpful to have a familiar communication partner act as a bridge between them and a communication partner who hasn’t encountered alternative ways of communicating before. The familiar communication partner might help to explain how the communication system works and redirect questions towards the individual to ensure they are included, for example. But this is entirely a matter of personal choice.
