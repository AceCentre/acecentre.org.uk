﻿When pointing to letters on an alphabet chart with a hand is difficult, one alternative is to use eye movement to point to letters. Although people can feel a bit worried about communicating in this way, it is easier than it looks. It can also be surprisingly quick.

See Getting started with eye pointing later on in this Chapter for ideas about introducing this strategy. There is also a useful section giving Tips for communication partners when communicating with someone who is eye pointing.

﻿Eye Pointing to Letters

Eye pointing to letters is often done using a Perspex® frame called an E-tran frame (short for eye transfer frame) that is held by the communication partner. However, eye pointing charts can easily be created that replicate the feel of an E-tran frame

An E-tran frame is a transparent Perspex® rectangle with a central window removed. The letters of the alphabet are attached to the frame. An eye pointing alphabet chart that functions like an E-tran frame can also be made by running a glossy laminate pouch through a laminator. See Chapter 13 or more information about making or sourcing E-tran frames.

An E-tran frame is designed to be held by the communication partner, although a well-positioned stand can be used. The E-tran frame is held so that the person eye pointing and their communication partner can make eye contact through the central window. The communication partner can see where on the frame the individual is looking and therefore work out what letter is being selected.
