Other Approaches to Encoded Eye Pointing Alphabet Charts﻿The ﻿Eye Talk, designed by KM CAT (Kent and Medway Communication and Assitive Technology Service) simplifies the process of encoding by separating out the steps involved. The eye pointing chart is spread out over a number of pages which are bound together. The individual first looks at the group that contains their target letter. The communication partner then turns to another page which only displays the letters within the selected group. They then eye point to their chosen letter. The resource can be downloaded here (select the Eye Pointing Resources option).

By contrast, Susan Stayte’s encoded eye pointing chart really pushes the boundaries of what you can achieve with a paper-based eye pointing chart. By combining encoded access to letters with coded access to words, she created a complex and sophisticated eye pointing chart for a very able young man who was confident in using his eyes to communicate.
