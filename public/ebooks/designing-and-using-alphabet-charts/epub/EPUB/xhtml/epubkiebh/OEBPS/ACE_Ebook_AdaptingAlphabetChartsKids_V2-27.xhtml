An alternative approach is to use a look-back-at-me strategy. This is where the individual holds their gaze briefly on the target letter then makes eye contact with the communication partner to signal that the last letter looked at was the selection. This can work well if eye movement is a little harder to read, or if the person looks around the frame or chart a lot, as it gives the communication partner some extra reassurance. Others prefer to look round, make eye contact with the communication partner to signal that they are ready to select a letter, then briefly hold their gaze on the target letter.

Whatever strategy is used, it is worth documenting it so that all know what is being done.

Such strategies often become redundant over time as all gain confidence and familiarity in communicating with eye movement.
