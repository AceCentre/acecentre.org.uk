The chapters so far have considered how the alphabet can be accessed using pointing, eye movement and listener mediated scanning. Some people choose to combine these access methods into one system.

In the example below, the individual might point to a group of letters using a hand or other part of their body and then use listener mediated scanning to identify their target letter within that group.
