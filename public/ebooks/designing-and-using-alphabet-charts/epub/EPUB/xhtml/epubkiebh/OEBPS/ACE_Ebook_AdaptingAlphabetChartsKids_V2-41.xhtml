Beatrice can vocalise or move her head to indicate ‘yes’ or ‘no’. She also uses a specific sideways head movement to indicate when a space is required. After letters that can potentially standalone, such as ‘A’ or ‘I’, Beatrice’s communication partners check whether or not the next letter selected is the start of a new word or the continuation of a word that begins with the letter ‘A’ or ‘I’.

The black letters on the board are written in permanent ink. As Beatrice selects a letter, her communication partner writes it on the whiteboard using a dry erase pen (blue ink in the photograph above). If the communication partner writes an incorrect letter, Beatrice will vocalise or move her head to indicate that things have gone wrong.

The black letters on Beatrice’s whiteboard are not in alphabetical order. They have been grouped according to frequency of use, with the goal of letters that are used more often in the English language (like ‘T’) being quicker to access than those that are used less often (like ‘Z’). You can find out more about arranging letters according to their frequency of use in Chapter 8.

This example is included to provide inspiration as to how varied paper-based communication tools can be. The key is to work out what sort of approach(es) best meets the needs of the individual.

To communicate using the whiteboard, Beatrice first eye points to the group of black letters that contains her target letter. Her communication partner then establishes if her letter is in the top or bottom row by asking ‘top row?’. If yes, they then use listener mediated scanning to offer the letters in top row, and if no, in the bottom row. However, familiar communication partners are cleverly able to ignore letters that can’t possibly follow on from the previous one selected e.g. in English, the letter ‘K’ never follows the letter ‘D’ within a word so they don’t bother offering that. This really helps to speed along communication. Just occasionally, they can even skip the top row question as there is only one possible letter in the block selected.
