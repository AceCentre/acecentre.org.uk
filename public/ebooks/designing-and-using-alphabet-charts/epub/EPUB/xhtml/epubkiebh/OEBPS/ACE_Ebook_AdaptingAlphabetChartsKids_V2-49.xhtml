Encoding groups letters together to reduce the number of target areas on an alphabet chart and was discussed earlier in relation to eye pointing. Encoded layouts are particularly beneficial for those accessing the alphabet with their eyes, and is the keyboard layout used by Tiago. However, encoded layouts are also used by people who like to point to an alphabet chart but find it difficult to be precise enough for a communication partner to decipher what is being pointed to.

Instead of pointing to one of twenty-six individual letters on an alphabet chart, with an encoded chart the individual points to the group of letters that contains their desired letter. They then make a second movement to clarify which of the letters in the group is the one they want to select.

Using an encoded layout can make it much easier for the communication partner to work out what is being selected. However, it does mean that the individual has to make two movements to select one letter.
