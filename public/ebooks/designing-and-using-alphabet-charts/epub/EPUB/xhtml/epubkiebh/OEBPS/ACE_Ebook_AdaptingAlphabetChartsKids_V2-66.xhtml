﻿﻿Letter Codes (also known as Abbreviation-expansion)

Letter codes, also known as abbreviation–expansion, represent a longer word or phrase with a short abbreviation. They may be used as an alternative to a communication chart or book, or alongside them.

Abbreviations are used in everyday language, particularly with the advent of quick messaging, and you may well have come across LOL being used for ‘laugh out loud’ or FYI for ‘for your information.’ However, some also choose to develop personalised letter codes to meet their own specific communication needs. For example, if someone was regularly spelling ‘Is there anything good on TV tonight’, they might decide to abbreviate this to something like GTV.

Some choose to use letter codes for just the odd phrase that they find themselves spelling a lot. However, others spend time developing a system of codes. A pet owner, for example, might choose to have a set of codes around animal care like OC for ‘Please let the cat out’, IC for ‘Please let the cat in’, FC for ‘Please feed the cat’ or perhaps LC for ‘leave the cat alone’.
